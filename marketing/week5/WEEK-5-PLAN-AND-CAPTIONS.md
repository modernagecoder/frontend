## 2. The Gauss trick (chalkboard)
**Headline:** Add every number from 1 to 100
**Caption:**
Add every number from 1 to 100. The slow way takes all day.

The clever way takes ten seconds. Pair the numbers: 1 and 100, 2 and 99, 3 and 98. Every pair makes 101, and there are 50 pairs. So it is 101 times 50, which is 5050. A schoolboy named Gauss worked this out in his head, and there are hundreds of little shortcuts like it.

We teach the thinking, not the grind. That is why maths stops feeling like a wall. Book a free demo, link in bio.

## 5. Fractions made obvious (diagram)
**Headline:** Fractions, made obvious.
**Caption:**
Here is why so many kids fall at fractions. School hands them the rule first, then wonders why it never sticks.

We do it backwards. Show three parts of four filled in, let them see it, feel that it is three quarters. Only then do we bring out the 3/4 and the rule, and now it actually means something. Same for decimals, percentages, the lot. See it first, name it second.

That is the whole method, and it works on the "scary" topics too. Book a free demo, link in bio.
