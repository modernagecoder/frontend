# ISEE and SSAT math prep: the tests that reward thinkers over drillers

**The 20-second answer:** build calculator-free number sense (neither test allows one), train the reasoning sections separately from the achievement sections, and teach strategy honestly: when to reason, when to estimate, when to skip.

## Know your test

ISEE (ERB): Lower/Middle/Upper levels; Quantitative Reasoning (word problems + quantitative comparisons at Middle/Upper) plus Mathematics Achievement; stanine 1-9 scoring against the applicant cohort; no wrong-answer penalty. SSAT: Elementary/Middle/Upper; two quantitative sections; percentile scoring; Middle/Upper have historically penalized wrong answers, making skip strategy a scored skill. Neither allows a calculator. We verify current policies at erblearn.org and ssat.org per test date.

## The signature skill

Quantitative comparison example: Column A = 47 x 53, Column B = 50 x 50. Both pairs sum to 100; of equal-sum pairs, the more balanced product wins. B, in ten seconds, no multiplication. Structure beats computation: that is the whole test philosophy, and it is teachable.

## Level map

Lower/Elementary: whole numbers, fractions, patterns, early word problems (mental-arithmetic speed hides the points). Middle: ratios, percents, integers, early algebra (QCs appear; estimation carries points). Upper: linear/quadratic algebra, functions, geometry, counting (skip discipline is worth real percentile).
