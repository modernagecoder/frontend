### Cuemath

Maths-only, live online · structured method

A dedicated maths platform built around its own visual, conceptual method, with live tutors and a clear progression. A strong choice if you want maths alone and value a fixed method. ### Vedantu

Live tutoring across subjects and boards

Broad live online tutoring that covers maths alongside science and board exam prep, with wide reach across Indian boards. Good for board-focused revision at scale. Batches can be larger, so a child who needs close individual attention may get less of it here than in a small group.

### BYJU'S

App-first · large content library

An enormous, polished library of recorded maths lessons and an app millions have used, with some live add-ons. The content depth is real. The well-documented trade-offs are that it is recorded-video first rather than live-mentor led, and it has drawn criticism for aggressive sales and large prepaid packages. Confirm exactly what is live before paying.

### Kumon

Worksheet self-study · maths and reading

A worksheet-based programme that builds daily calculation fluency and discipline through repetition, offline and online. It genuinely builds speed and consistency. It is not live-mentor teaching and does little for conceptual understanding or problem solving, so it suits fluency drills more than deep maths.

### Mathnasium

Maths-only tutoring · centre-based, some online

A maths-only tutoring service, traditionally at physical centres with some online options, using a diagnostic approach to target gaps. Strong for personalised remediation. It is maths only, often centre-based, and typically premium-priced, which suits families near a centre who want focused maths help.

What is the best age to start maths tuition?

Structured online maths tuition works well from about age six, once a child can engage with a teacher for a full lesson. Earlier than that, playful number sense at home matters more. The strongest signal is not age but whether the child needs support or stretch right now.

What should a good online maths class include?

A live teacher who explains and corrects in real time; small batches; a focus on understanding and problem solving rather than only drilling; alignment to your board; and enough frequency for momentum. Bonus: maths connected to coding, which turns abstract concepts into things a child can build.

Which maths option is best value?

Value depends on understanding gained per hour, not just price. Live small-batch teaching that builds real understanding beats cheap self-study worksheets or expensive one-to-one you do not need.
