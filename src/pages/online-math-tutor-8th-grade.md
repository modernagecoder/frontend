# Online math tutor for 8th grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 8 tutor builds slope as a rate the student can see (growing out of grade 7 proportionality), makes functions concrete as machines before notation abstracts them, and gets equation-solving industrial-strength with variables on both sides.

## Why grade 8 is the densest year

Functions organize algebra through calculus; the line is the benchmark every later curve gets compared to. Around them: exponent laws, scientific notation, irrationals, Pythagoras, transformations, volumes, scatter plots. The common invisible failure: computing slope from a formula without knowing what slope means. Formula-holders survive grade 8 and drown in Algebra 2.

## Signs an 8th grader needs help

Slope is a formula, not a meaning; x squared times x cubed = x to the sixth; variables on both sides trigger improvisation; functions are "the f(x) thing"; Pythagoras applied to any triangle; scientific-notation signs scramble; graphs read but not interpreted; placement pressure; eroding confidence.

## The complete grade 8 map (Common Core aligned)

- Number System: irrational numbers, approximating roots by squeezing.
- Expressions and Equations: exponent laws with expansion proofs, square/cube roots, scientific notation with meaning, proportional vs linear (y = mx vs y = mx + b), equations with variables on both sides including no-solution cases, systems of two equations.
- Functions: the one-output rule, comparing functions across representations, linear vs nonlinear, modeling with rate and initial value.
- Geometry: transformations and which preserve size, congruence and similarity via transformations, transversal angle rules, Pythagorean theorem from areas plus converse and coordinate distance, volumes of cylinders/cones/spheres.
- Statistics: scatter plots, informal trend lines interpreted in context, two-way tables.

## School-year pacing

Aug-Oct exponents and scientific notation; Oct-Dec equations both sides; Dec-Feb slope, y = mx + b, functions (the heart of the year); Feb-Mar systems and scatter plots; Mar-May geometry; May-Jun review, testing, placement.
