## Where each one genuinely wins

Kumon has stayed popular for six decades for real reasons. Here is the fair trade-off.

### Where Kumon is a better fit

- **Building a daily habit.** The worksheet-a-day structure builds discipline and routine like almost nothing else.
- **Fluency through repetition.** For pure calculation speed, drilling works, and Kumon has refined it since 1958.
- **Very young starters.** Kumon takes children as young as three; our live classes start at age six.
- **Your child self-motivates.** Kids who enjoy working alone through progressive levels can move fast in the method.

### Where Modern Age Coders wins

- **Someone actually teaches.** A live mentor explains the concept, spots the misunderstanding, and fixes it in the same hour.
- **Understanding over speed.** We aim at the why behind the maths, then apply it in projects and code, not just faster arithmetic.
- **One price, everything included.** $100 to $150 a month all-in versus per-subject fees plus registration and assessment costs.
- **Coding under the same roof.** The same school grows both skills, with maths feeding directly into programming.
- **No worksheet battles at home.** Parents of Kumon kids know the daily packet struggle; our practice is project-driven.

## Frequently asked questions

Is Modern Age Coders cheaper than Kumon?

Usually, yes, and the difference grows with each subject. Kumon typically costs $150 to $220 per subject per month in the US as of July 2026, so maths plus reading commonly reaches $300 to $400, on top of a registration fee of about $50. Modern Age Coders maths is $100 a month for small groups or $150 for 1-on-1, all-inclusive, with 8 live 60-minute classes a month and no registration fee.

Is Kumon a class or worksheets?

Kumon is a worksheet method by design. Children work through daily packets mostly on their own, with short check-ins at the center rather than taught lessons. That builds discipline and calculation fluency, but it is not the same as a teacher explaining concepts live. Modern Age Coders is the opposite model: every session is a live, taught class with a mentor who knows your child.

Which is better for a child who is behind in maths?

It depends on why they are behind. If the gap is practice and fluency, Kumon's repetition can close it. If the gap is understanding, a worksheet cannot answer a question, and that is where live teaching matters: our mentors find the exact misunderstanding and fix it in conversation. Our free demo class is a low-risk way to see whether your child responds to being taught live.

Does Kumon teach coding?

No, Kumon covers maths and reading only. Modern Age Coders teaches coding and maths, and many of our students use maths inside their coding projects, which is often the moment the subject finally clicks.

What does Modern Age Coders cost, exactly?

For international students, maths is $100 a month in a small group or $150 a month 1-on-1, and coding is $40 a month in a group or $100 1-on-1. Every plan is 2 live classes a week, 8 a month, with no registration fee and monthly billing you can stop anytime. Every student starts with a free demo class.
