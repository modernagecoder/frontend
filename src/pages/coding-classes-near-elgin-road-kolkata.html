LIVE ONLINE · PREMIUM QUALITY

What "premium and live" actually means here

Every Modern Age Coders class near Elgin Road is genuinely live and premium — taught in real time by a full-time engineer or maths graduate to a batch of six to eight students, with screens shared both ways, instant feedback and project-based work. There are no pre-recorded videos, no rotating part-time tutors, and no oversized batches where a quiet child disappears. This is the standard the La Martiniere belt expects, delivered to a laptop at home.

Live, never pre-recorded

Classes happen at a fixed time with a real teacher who can see your child's code as they type it. When a La Martiniere student gets stuck on a BlueJ loop or a Python list, the correction happens in that minute — not in a comment thread the next day. Recorded lessons can supplement, but they never replace the live class.

Small batches of 6–8

A maximum of eight students per batch means every child speaks, codes and is questioned in every session. For families wanting the academic intensity of a Loreto or St. Xavier's classroom, the mini batch (3–4) or 1-on-1 personalised classes bring the ratio close to private tuition without losing the energy of peers.

Taught by full-time engineers & maths graduates

Teachers are full-time professionals — working engineers and mathematics graduates — not college students taking evening shifts. They have built real software and solved real maths, so they can stretch a child who already tops their class and answer the fact-checking questions Elgin Road parents are known for.

Project-based, not worksheet-based

Children build things they keep: a working web app, a Python tool, a quiz, a small game. A Class 8 student near Netaji Bhawan built an interactive heritage map; a Class 10 student wrote a duty-roster tool her mother now uses at Belle Vue. Projects make abstract syllabus concepts concrete and give pupils a portfolio.

Syllabus-aware for ICSE, CBSE & ISC

The curriculum maps onto what your child already studies. The Java/ICSE track follows the Class 9–10 Computer Applications (BlueJ) syllabus including project files and viva; the board CS course covers Class 11–12 Computer Science and Informatics Practices for both Python (CBSE) and Java (ISC).

No Elgin Road commute

On a school evening the crossing past Forum onto AJC Bose Road is a standstill of buses, autos and school cars, and changing autos at Hazra or Beck Bagan adds another leg. Live online erases roughly 90 minutes of that daily round-trip, so a child can start the moment they are home — calmer, on time, with energy left to learn.

LIVE ONLINE vs OFFLINE

An honest comparison for Elgin Road

Where live online wins

  • No evening crawl past Forum onto AJC Bose Road — roughly 90 minutes saved every class day.
  • No changing autos at Hazra or Beck Bagan; your child starts the minute they are home.
  • Screen-share both ways means the teacher sees the actual code and corrects it live.
  • Access to full-time engineers and maths graduates, not just whoever lives near Elgin Road.
  • Recordings of your own class to revise from, plus shared project files you keep.
  • Small batches of 6–8 hold the same intensity online — every child is seen and questioned.
  • Easy to slot around heavy ICSE tuition, school tests and exam weeks.

When offline still wins

  • Very young first-timers who still need a parent physically beside the keyboard.
  • Families who specifically want a same-room social setting over academic depth.
  • Homes with genuinely unreliable internet or no quiet corner to work in.
  • Learners who simply focus better with a teacher in the same physical room.

For the academically driven, time-pressed families of the Elgin Road belt, live online wins on almost every count that matters — and a free demo lets you judge it for yourself before paying anything.
