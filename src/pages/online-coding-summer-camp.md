# Online coding summer camp: camp-level fun, school-year-level skill

**The 20-second answer:** real live teaching (not videos with a camp logo), a project the child ships by August, and scheduling that survives family vacations — with the option to simply keep the mentor in September.

## Why spacing beats the camp week

One intense week makes memories; regular sessions spaced over weeks with the same teacher make skills — the way sports coaches and music teachers have always worked. Two 1-hour classes weekly all summer, slots moving with your trips, recordings covering misses.

## The four tracks

First Games (Scratch, 6-9) → a real chase/platformer game. Game Builders (9-12) → a published Roblox/Minecraft/Scratch piece. Python Start (10-14) → quiz game, generative art or Minecraft-by-Python. AI Explorers (11-16) → chatbots and image tools with honest how-it-works.

## Honest timing

Early summer = full arc. Mid-summer = compressed but 12 taught hours still beats any camp week. Late summer = pre-season training for September clubs. Not summer = same classes run all year; camp is our regular teaching wearing sunscreen.
