## A real worked example: differentiation from first principles

A derivative is the gradient of a curve at a point, found by letting two points slide together:

- gradient between x and x+h: (f(x+h) − f(x)) / h
- let h → 0: f′(x) = lim (f(x+h) − f(x)) / h
- for f(x) = x²: ((x+h)² − x²)/h = 2x + h → **2x**

The power rule is just what this limit gives. Seen as "instantaneous rate of change",
first principles, tangents, rates and optimisation become one idea — understanding that
carries into university calculus.
