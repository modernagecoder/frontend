# Online math tutor for 3rd grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 3 tutor gives you a real teacher live for a full hour (not an app), teaching that builds understanding of multiplication, division and fractions, and the same mentor every class. That is what we do.

## Why grade 3 matters

Third grade is the year math stops being counting. Multiplication asks children to think in groups; fractions introduce numbers between the numbers they know, the biggest conceptual jump of elementary school. A struggling third grader is almost never "bad at math": they are missing one or two specific ideas, and every lesson after feels like noise.

## Signs a 3rd grader needs help

Counts on fingers for everything; multiplication is pure memorization; word problems cause instant guessing; "I hate math" appears; fractions cause panic; homework takes an hour in tears; fine on worksheets but lost elsewhere; teacher said "needs support"; rushes or freezes.

## How we teach: concrete, pictorial, abstract

Multiplication starts as real groups the child can move, becomes a drawn array and bar model, and only then 4 x 6 = 24. Live and interactive the whole hour; one dedicated mentor; confidence engineered on purpose.

## The complete grade 3 map (Common Core aligned)

- Operations and Algebraic Thinking: meaning of multiplication and division, fluency within 100, two-step word problems, properties, patterns.
- Number and Operations in Base Ten: place value to 1,000, rounding, add/subtract within 1,000 with regrouping, multiply by multiples of 10.
- Fractions: unit fractions, fractions on the number line, equivalence, comparison, whole numbers as fractions.
- Measurement and Data: time to the minute, volume and mass, scaled graphs, area, perimeter.
- Geometry: shape categories, partitioning into equal areas.

## School-year pacing (typical US classroom)

Aug-Oct place value and regrouping; Oct-Dec meaning of multiplication and division; Jan-Feb fluency and two-step problems; Feb-Apr fractions (the hardest stretch, before state tests); Apr-May area, perimeter, time, graphs; May-Jun review and testing.

## Common misconceptions we fix

"Multiplication makes bigger"; "the bigger denominator wins"; "subtract the smaller digit"; "area and perimeter are the same"; "the answer is the two numbers combined"; "zero does nothing".
