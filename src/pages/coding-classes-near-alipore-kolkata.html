WHY MODERN AGE CODERS

Premium live online — built for Alipore rigour

Modern Age Coders is a premium live online coding and maths school: every class is taught in real time by a full-time engineer or maths graduate, in a small batch of 6–8, with the child writing and running real code on screen. It is the opposite of the recorded-video-and-worksheet model — and it is built for Alipore families who already pay for serious private tuition and expect rigour.

Live, never recorded

Every session is a real, scheduled live class — your child speaks to the teacher, asks questions and gets answers in the moment. Nothing is a pre-recorded playlist left to watch alone. If a concept does not land, the instructor re-explains it on the spot, that same evening.

Batches of 6–8, not 40

Group batches are capped at 6–8 students so every child is known by name and gets airtime each class. For board years and intensive needs there is a 3–4 mini batch or full 1-on-1. This is the small-room rigour Alipore families expect from a good home tutor — with a wider curriculum behind it.

Taught by people who ship code

Instructors are full-time software engineers and maths graduates who write production code and solve real problems, not part-time readers of a slide deck. Your child learns current, working practice — how code is actually built, debugged and shipped — rather than a textbook frozen a decade ago.

Project-based, portfolio-first

Children build real, working things — a Python tracker, a BlueJ Java program, a small web app — not just notes. Every term leaves a tangible portfolio piece the child can show, run and explain. Learning sticks because it produced something real.

Board-aware curriculum

The coding syllabus maps cleanly onto the ISC, ICSE, CBSE and IGCSE Computer Science and Computer Applications courses taught at local schools. BlueJ Java for ICSE, Python for CBSE — board practicals and project files are covered directly, not as an afterthought.

Zero commute, full attention

Classes run live from home, so the 30–45 minute crawl down Alipore Road and the Diamond Harbour Road signal, plus the parking fight near the Zoo, simply disappear. That recovered ~90 minutes per class day goes back into study, rest or family — without losing any of the live, supervised teaching.

THE ALIPORE STORY

Old-money rigour, no glossy ed-tech pitch

Alipore is South Kolkata's quiet, old-money address — a low-rise belt of judges' bungalows, IAS and IPS quarters and multi-generational doctor and industrialist households around Judges Court Road and Burdwan Road, in the shadow of the National Library at Belvedere Estate and the Zoological Garden. It is un-flashy by choice. Families here value rigour and discretion over dashboards and gamified badges, and most already keep a private home tutor on retainer.

That is exactly the family we are built for. A single home tutor knows your child but carries one person's knowledge; our live batches of 6–8 give the same small-room attention while putting a full-time engineer or maths graduate — someone who ships real code — in front of your child. The curriculum is wider, the teaching is current, and the standard is the rigour Alipore expects, not a glossy ed-tech pitch.

And it happens without anyone leaving the house. Evening Alipore Road, Judges Court Road and the Diamond Harbour Road signal at Belvedere take 30–45 minutes to crawl through on a tuition-centre run, with a parking fight near the Zoo and National Library waiting at the other end. Live online removes roughly 90 minutes of round-trip commute on every class day — time that stays with study, rest and the family.

HONEST COMPARISON

Where live online wins — and where it does not

Where live online wins

  • Zero commute — no 30–45 minute crawl down Alipore Road or the Diamond Harbour Road signal at Belvedere, and no parking fight near the Zoo.
  • Roughly 90 minutes per class day recovered for study, rest and family.
  • Access to full-time engineers and maths graduates who ship real code, not just whoever is local.
  • Small live batches of 6–8 — the same attention as a home tutor, with a wider curriculum.
  • Every class is screen-shared and interactive; your child codes live, not from the back of a room.
  • Sessions can be reviewed and missed classes made up — no lost evening, no lost lesson.
  • Safe, supervised and at home — no evening travel through busy South Kolkata roads.

When offline still wins (honest)

  • A child who genuinely cannot focus without a teacher physically in the room.
  • Households without a reliable broadband connection or a quiet corner for class.
  • Very young children who still need an adult beside them to operate the device.
  • Families who specifically want in-person social grouping over curriculum depth.

For most Alipore families, live online delivers the rigour of a good home tutor plus a deeper curriculum and zero commute — but we will tell you honestly if offline suits your child better.
