Behind the number

What actually drives the price

1

Real estate and overheads

Centers pass on rent and franchise fees. A $350 membership is partly a lease payment; none of it reaches your child's understanding.

2

Tutor labour market

US-based tutors price against US wages. Live online schools teaching globally pay excellent teachers well in their own economies, which is why depth can cost less.

3

Format efficiency

1-on-1 costs more than groups; scheduled programs cost less per hour than ad-hoc bookings. Frequency matters more than families expect: two classes a week compounds, one does not.

4

What you are actually buying

An hour of drilling, an hour on a learning floor, and an hour of deep interactive teaching are all sold as tutoring. They are not the same product.

5

Fees around the fee

Enrollment charges ($99 to $200 at centers), registration fees, assessment costs and long-plan lock-ins can add 10 to 20 percent to year-one cost.

6

Grade level

Elementary tutoring runs cheaper ($23 to $75/hr) than high school ($30 to $100/hr); calculus and test prep sit at the top of every range.

Honest answers

Frequently asked questions

How much should I pay for a math tutor per hour?

In the US in 2026, $25 to $80 an hour covers most competent private tutors; under $20 usually means a student tutor or a global online school, and above $80 buys specialist credentials or test-prep branding. Price the outcome, not the hour: two focused hours a week at $15 each will usually beat one $80 hour every other week.

Are monthly programs better value than hourly tutors?

Usually, if your child needs sustained help rather than exam-week rescue. Monthly structures create rhythm and accountability, and per-hour cost drops: Cuemath works out around $25 to $32 a class, Modern Age Coders at $12.50 to $18.75. Hourly booking wins only for short-term, targeted gaps.

Why are live online schools so much less expensive?

No real estate, no franchise fees, and teachers hired in economies where excellent teaching pays well below US rates. The hour itself, a dedicated mentor teaching deeply and interactively, is the same product US families pay $60+ for locally. That is a structural difference, not a quality discount.

How do I judge a tutor before paying?

Watch them teach. One real lesson reveals more than any pricing page: does the tutor explain the why, ask your child questions, adapt in real time, and leave them wanting the next class? Our demo class is free precisely so you can apply that test to us first.
