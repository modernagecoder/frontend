Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our project-based learning approach

What projects can beginners build after learning to code? +
Beginners can build exciting projects like word counters, image sliders, memory games (like Simon Game), and simple calculators. As they progress, they move on to weather apps, typing challenges, and interactive websites. Our students typically build their first project within 2-3 months of starting.
How long does it take to build a complete project? +
Simple projects like a word counter or image slider take 1-2 weeks. Intermediate projects like weather apps or food trackers take 3-4 weeks. Advanced projects like AI chatbots, brain-training games, or full-stack platforms can take 2-3 months. Our instructors guide students through every step.
Do students get certificates for their projects? +
Yes! Students receive a project completion certificate for every major project they build. These certificates can be added to college applications, LinkedIn profiles, and resumes. We also showcase the best projects here on Student Labs.
What technologies will students learn to build these projects? +
Students learn industry-standard technologies including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, React, Node.js, MongoDB, Firebase, and AI/ML frameworks. The specific tech stack depends on the course and project chosen.
Can my child's project be featured on Student Labs? +
Absolutely! Any student enrolled in our courses can submit their completed project for review. Projects that demonstrate creativity, technical skill, and completeness are featured on this page with the student's name and a live link to their project.
What age groups can participate in project-based learning? +
Our project-based learning is designed for all ages — kids (6-12), teens (13-18), college students, and working professionals. Projects are tailored to each age group's skill level, from Scratch games for young kids to full-stack AI applications for advanced learners.
