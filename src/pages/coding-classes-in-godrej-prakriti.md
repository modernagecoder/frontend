## How we teach: fundamentals first, tools second, understanding always

Every class is led live by an instructor who can see your child's screen and work.

1. A real teacher on a live screen. No pre-recorded playlists. Your child writes code while a teacher watches, asks questions and corrects mistakes the moment they happen — without anyone leaving the township.
2. By hand first, shortcuts later. Students build something themselves before they are handed the ready-made shortcut, so they understand what the shortcut is really doing.
3. Maths and code that lean on each other. Loops meet the multiplication tables; functions meet algebra; coordinates meet graphs — the school maths a Sodepur child studies is tied to the code they write in class.
4. Progress a parent can follow. You see what was taught this week and what comes next. Every session is recorded, so a missed Saturday or a tricky topic can be replayed before the next class.

### The AI Trap

AI tools can write code, but they cannot understand on your child's behalf. In our classes we set up deliberate moments where an AI answer looks right and is quietly wrong, and the student has to catch it. A child who owns the fundamentals can use AI as a tool and still know when it is lying. That judgement is the real skill we are building.
