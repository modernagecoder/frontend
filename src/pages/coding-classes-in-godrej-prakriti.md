How we teach

## Fundamentals first. Tools second. Understanding always.

Every class is led live by an instructor who can see your child's screen and work. Here is what that looks like, week to week, for a learner at Godrej Prakriti.

01

### A real teacher, on a live screen

No pre-recorded playlists left running while a child loses interest. Your son or daughter writes code while a teacher watches, asks questions, and corrects mistakes the moment they happen — the way honest tuition has always worked, only without anyone leaving the township.

02

### By hand first, shortcuts later

We ask students to build something themselves before they are handed the ready-made shortcut. A child who has sorted a list by hand understands what a one-line sort actually does. The shortcut becomes a reward for understanding, not a substitute for it.

03

### Maths and code that lean on each other

Loops meet the multiplication tables; functions meet algebra; coordinates meet graphs. We deliberately tie the school maths a Sodepur child is already studying to the code they write in class, so each subject makes the other feel easier.

04

### Progress a parent can actually follow

You see what was taught this week and what comes next. Every session is recorded, so a missed Saturday, an exam week or a tricky topic can be replayed at home before the following class — nobody comes back lost.

The AI Trap

### We show students where AI fails — on purpose.

AI tools can write code, but they cannot understand on your child's behalf. In our classes we set up deliberate moments where an AI answer looks right and is quietly wrong, and the student has to catch it. A child who only copies from a chatbot freezes the moment a problem turns unfamiliar. A child who owns the fundamentals can use AI as a tool and still know when it is lying. That judgement is the real skill we are building.
