ONLINE vs OFFLINE

An honest comparison

Where live online wins

  • No commute through Rashbehari crossing or the Gariahat flyover bottleneck — roughly 90 minutes saved every class day.
  • Genuinely small live batches of 6–8, instead of crowded 20-student tuition rooms off Rashbehari Avenue.
  • Taught by full-time engineers and maths graduates, not whoever was free that evening locally.
  • Your child shares their screen, so the teacher sees exactly where they are stuck and fixes it live.
  • Sessions can be revised and recapped; lessons are structured and project-based, not ad hoc.
  • One household can run two boards at once — CBSE Python and ICSE Java — without two separate coaching trips.
  • Safer evenings: no auto-rickshaw queues at Lake Market or Gariahat after dark.

When offline still wins (honestly)

  • A very young child who genuinely cannot sit at a screen yet may do better in person for a while.
  • Families who specifically want a physical room and face-to-face supervision over flexibility.
  • Homes with no reliable internet connection or no quiet corner to learn from.
  • Learners who need hands-on hardware or lab equipment that a software class does not require.

For most coding and maths learning, live online with a small batch and a real teacher delivers more attention and better results than the average neighbourhood centre — and gives your child their evenings back.
