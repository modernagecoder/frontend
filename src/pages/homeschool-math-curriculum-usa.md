## The problem we solve

You can teach almost everything yourself, but math is uniquely demanding: it builds strictly
on itself, a single missed idea quietly breaks everything after, and the upper levels ask
for expertise most parents last used decades ago. Boxed curricula march every child at the
same pace through the same order — the exact thing you left the classroom to escape.
Homeschool math should be fully individual, and a dedicated 1:1 mentor makes that possible.

## How it works

1. **Place by understanding** — find what your child genuinely knows, often different across topics.
2. **Design a scope-and-sequence** — a coherent route, Common Core-aligned or standards-independent.
3. **Teach for mastery, not the calendar** — linger where needed, accelerate where ready.
4. **Keep you informed and on the record** — notes and summaries for portfolios and evaluations.

## A real worked example: a custom scope-and-sequence

A "5th-grade" homeschooler flies through multiplication but stalls on fractions. Instead of
one rigid grade level, we sequence each strand on its own:

- multiplication/division → on track, push into early algebra thinking
- fractions → stop and rebuild: what a fraction *is*, on a number line
- then → equivalence, comparing, adding, once meaning is solid
- reassess in ~3 weeks → rejoin the strands when fractions are secure

A classroom or boxed curriculum can't hold one strand and repair it while another keeps
flying. A 1:1 mentor can — and that flexibility, with a record of what's mastered, is what
good homeschool math looks like.
