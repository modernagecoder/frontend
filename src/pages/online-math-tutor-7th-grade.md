# Online math tutor for 7th grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 7 tutor builds proportional reasoning as one idea (so percent, scale, rates and later slope click into the same slot), derives the sign rules from patterns instead of chants, and gets two-step equations fluent before algebra assumes them.

## Why grade 7 predicts algebra

Slope, similar triangles, unit conversion, percent change, trig ratios: all proportional reasoning, and grade 7 is where that move becomes reasoning or hardens into cross-multiplication ritual. Grade 7 also finishes the number system, and every algebra error chain traces back through the sign rules. Many US districts decide algebra-in-grade-8 placement on grade 7 performance.

## Signs a 7th grader needs help

Every proportion cross-multiplied blind; sign rules scramble across operations; reverse percent problems are a wall; two-step equations get improvised; circumference and area formulas swap; fractions in equations trigger panic; probability answered by vibes; correct answers dissolve under "why?"; placement looming.

## The complete grade 7 map (Common Core aligned)

- Ratios and Proportional Relationships: unit rates with fractions, testing for proportionality, constant of proportionality (y = kx), percent in all directions (discount, markup, tax, tip, commission, simple interest, percent error).
- The Number System: rational addition/subtraction as moves on the line, multiplication/division with derived sign rules, terminating vs repeating decimals, complex expressions.
- Expressions and Equations: like terms with rational coefficients, two-step equations px + q = r by balance, two-step inequalities with the flip explained, modeling word problems.
- Geometry: scale drawings, angle relationships (vertical, supplementary, complementary), circles (where 2 pi r and pi r squared come from), area/surface area/volume, cross-sections.
- Statistics and Probability: random sampling, comparing populations, probability models, compound events with trees and tables.
