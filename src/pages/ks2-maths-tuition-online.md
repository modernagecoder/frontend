## A real worked example: why long division works

Division is sharing into equal groups, one place value at a time. For 432 ÷ 3:

- share 4 hundreds into 3 → 1 each, 1 hundred (= 10 tens) left over
- 13 tens into 3 → 4 each, 1 ten (= 10 ones) left over
- 12 ones into 3 → 4 each → **144**

The "bring down" step is just carrying a leftover into the next place value — like sharing
out money. Long division stops being a fragile ritual and becomes something a child
understands and can check. That's a SATs score that holds up versus a coin-flip.
