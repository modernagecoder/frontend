Why Heritage School Students Need a Coding & Maths Edge

The Heritage School isn't an ordinary school — and its students shouldn't settle for ordinary after-school classes. Here's the context that shapes everything we teach.

India's Only School Running Three Boards Simultaneously

Founded in 2001 by the Kalyan Bharti Trust and nestled on Chowbaga Road in Anandapur, The Heritage School is one of the very few institutions in India that simultaneously offers ICSE, Cambridge IGCSE, and the IB Diploma Programme — alongside NIOS for inclusive education. With ~3,200 students and alumni at IITs, Stanford, Oxford, Cambridge, and UC Berkeley, Heritage is not just a school; it is a launchpad for global ambition. Our curriculum at Modern Age Coders is built to serve all three tracks — so whether your child is in the ICSE stream, preparing for Cambridge A-Levels via IGCSE, or navigating the demanding IBDP, we have a precisely tailored programme for them. No generic content. No one-size-fits-all syllabi. Pure Heritage-specific mentorship.

The Tri-Board Academic Pressure No One Prepares You For

ICSE students at Heritage face some of India's most rigorous board exams — known for exhaustive syllabi, application-heavy questions, and no room for rote memorisation. IGCSE students are benchmarked against global peers in Singapore and the UK. IBDP students navigate Internal Assessments, a 4,000-word Extended Essay, Theory of Knowledge, and six demanding subject exams — while maintaining a CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) portfolio. Computer Science and Mathematics are central to all three. An ICSE student needs to ace Computer Applications with Python. An IGCSE student needs to master Cambridge Computer Science (0478). An IBDP student needs to write an Extended Essay in Computer Science and tackle IBDP HL Mathematics. We map every module we teach to these exact requirements — so every hour your child spends with us directly improves their board results.

The IIT–Oxford–Stanford Multiplier: Why Coding + Maths Changes Everything

Heritage regularly sends students to IIT, BITS Pilani, IIM, Stanford, Oxford, and UC Berkeley. Ananya Maity became the ISC national topper — the best in all of India — from this very campus. These outcomes are no accident. Heritage students are trained to think independently, research deeply, and question everything. When we combine that mindset with coding fluency and mathematical mastery, the results are exceptional. A student who learns sorting algorithms in Python simultaneously builds the abstract reasoning that cracks JEE. A student who writes an AI project for their IBDP Extended Essay builds a university application portfolio that stands out at Oxford. A student who trains for ZCO and INOI joins an elite global cohort. We don't teach coding and maths in isolation — we teach them as compounding investments in your child's future.

Batches Built Around the Heritage School Calendar

Heritage students have packed schedules — school hours, activities, sports, and Heritage's own enrichment clubs and community service programmes. We know your child is juggling more than most. That's why we offer flexible evening batches (4 PM – 8 PM on weekdays) and full weekend options (Saturday & Sunday, 9 AM – 7 PM). All classes are live and online — students connect from home near Anandapur, East Kolkata Township, Kasba, Ballygunge, Jadavpur, Golf Green, or anywhere in the city with zero commute. Sessions are recorded so not a single class is ever missed. Families from as far as Salt Lake and New Town choose us because of this flexibility. The new Hemanta Mukhopadhyay metro station at Ruby Crossing makes our occasional in-person workshops accessible too — but our primary format remains live online, always.

Questions from Heritage School Parents

Everything you need to know before booking your first class.

Do you offer classes specifically for Heritage School students? +
We are not affiliated with The Heritage School, but our classes are extremely popular among Heritage students across all three academic tracks. We align our curriculum with ICSE, Cambridge IGCSE, and IBDP — so every student gets precisely relevant content. We track Heritage's internal exam schedule and adjust pacing so your child is always ahead of assessments.
How do you support Heritage's IBDP students specifically? +
For IBDP students at Heritage, we offer dedicated support for IBDP Computer Science (HL and SL) and IBDP Mathematics (AA and AI, both HL and SL). This includes Internal Assessment mentorship, Extended Essay guidance in Computer Science, and structured coverage of all IB exam components including Paper 1, 2, and 3 (HL). We understand how the IB marks scheme works — which most tutors don't.
Do you prepare Heritage students for ZCO and INOI Coding Olympiads? +
Yes — we have a dedicated Competitive Programming track for Heritage students aspiring to qualify for the Zonal Computing Olympiad (ZCO), Indian National Olympiad in Informatics (INOI), and eventually the IOI. The track covers algorithms, data structures, dynamic programming, graph theory, and timed mock contests in the ZCO format. We recommend starting this track from Class 7 or 8 for best results.
What batches are available near Anandapur and East Kolkata Township? +
All classes are live online, so there's no commute from Anandapur, Kasba, Ballygunge, Jadavpur, or anywhere in Kolkata. We offer weekday evening batches (4 PM – 8 PM) and full weekend batches (Saturday & Sunday, 9 AM – 7 PM). Every session is recorded — so Heritage students who miss a class due to school events or co-curriculars can simply watch the recording. Nothing is ever lost.
At what age can my child start? Which course is right for Class 4? +
Children can start from age 6 (Pre-Primary / Class 1) with our Scratch block-coding programme. For Class 4–5, we recommend Scratch advanced or beginner HTML/CSS. From Class 6, Python is the natural progression. By Class 8–9, students are ready for AI/ML projects and board-aligned deep Python. We'll suggest the ideal starting point in your free demo class.
How much do coding and maths classes cost? +
Group classes (4–6 students) start at ₹1499/month for 2 live sessions per week. Mini Batch (3-4 students) at ₹2499/month or Personalised 1:1 classes at ₹4999/month. There is always a free demo class before any commitment — no payment required for the demo. We also offer bundled coding + maths packages at a combined rate.
Do you offer summer coding camps for Heritage students? +
Yes! We run intensive summer camps during April–May and special Durga Puja holiday camps in October. Heritage School students can complete a full course module in 3–4 accelerated weeks. Past themes include Python & AI, Web App Development, Game Dev, and Competitive Programming Bootcamp. Contact us for 2026 summer camp dates and early-bird pricing.
Will coding help my Heritage child in ICSE Computer Applications? +
Significantly. Our Python courses map directly to the ICSE Computer Applications syllabus (Java + Python options). Students who learn with us consistently outperform in both the theory and practical components of their ICSE board exams. For IGCSE students, we cover the full Cambridge Computer Science (0478/0984) specification. Students don't just memorise syntax — they understand it deeply enough to tackle any question the board can throw at them.
Can I see how you teach before paying for classes? +
Yes — and we encourage it. Visit our free How We Teach demo library to watch real, full-length class recordings for both coding and maths, organised by topic and age. There is no sign-up and no payment. You will see our exact teaching style before your child's free demo class — so you know precisely what you are enrolling into.
