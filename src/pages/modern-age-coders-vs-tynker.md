# Modern Age Coders vs Tynker

**In one paragraph:** Pick Tynker as a first taste: a well-made gamified app for a curious 5 to 7 year old exploring block coding alone. Pick Modern Age Coders when you want your child actually taught: a live mentor who explains deeply, corrects in real time, sets real projects and keeps them accountable, in interactive one-hour classes children look forward to.

## Where Tynker genuinely wins

- A low-stakes first taste for ages 5 to 7.
- Self-motivated tinkerers who enjoy working alone.
- Family plan covers up to 3 kids.
- No schedule at all.

## Where Modern Age Coders wins

- Someone is actually teaching: deep, interactive, adjusted to your child in real time.
- Real projects with human engineering feedback (see /student-labs).
- Progress that does not stall in month two.
- Maths grows alongside the code, one school for both.
- One full interactive hour, twice a week: classes children wait for.

## What the money buys

Tynker: ~$180/year of software access, no teacher. MAC: 8 live taught hours a month from $40. The comparison is not price; it is what the money buys.
