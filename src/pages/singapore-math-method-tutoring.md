## Bar model in action

"Maya and Jon have $120 altogether; Maya has $30 more." Draw Jon as a bar, Maya as the same bar plus $30. Two equal bars = $90, Jon = $45. No algebra, yet the drawing IS 2x + 30 = 120, met four years early. Upper-stage before-and-after ratio problems solve in ten seconds of drawing.

## The mastery ladder

Early (K-2): number bonds, part-whole thinking. Middle (3-4): multiplication/division mastery, fractions as numbers, bar models arrive. Upper (5-6): ratio, percentage, multi-step models. Bridge (6-8): models translated into equations side by side, so algebra arrives as notation for owned ideas.
