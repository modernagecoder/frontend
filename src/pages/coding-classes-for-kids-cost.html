The 20-second answer

Kids coding in the US in 2026 spans three product types with very different economics: apps (about $15 a month, no teacher), marketplaces ($10 to $30 per one-off class hour), and structured programs with real teachers ($160 to $300 a month). The thing to price is not the subscription, it is the taught hour: what a live mentor actually teaching your child costs per class.

Behind the number

What actually drives the price

1

Teacher or no teacher

Apps cost less because nobody is teaching. The moment a real human explains, corrects and challenges, you are paying for their hour.

2

Where the teachers live

US-based instructor programs price against US wages, roughly $70 a class. Global live-online schools deliver the same dedicated hour from a different cost base.

3

Frequency

Coding is momentum-driven. Four classes a month keeps a project alive; eight makes it compound. Compare programs per month of classes, not per label.

4

Structure versus sampling

One-off marketplace classes look cheap per hour, but a curriculum where hour 30 builds on hour 29 is what produces real ability.

5

Class size

1-on-1 costs most, small groups sit in the middle, and large webinars are cheap because your child is an audience member, not a student.

6

What is bundled

Maths, project reviews, recordings, and mentor continuity are worth real money when included, and cost extra everywhere they are not.

Frequently asked questions

How much should coding classes for a 10 year old cost?

For real, live teaching in 2026: $40 to $160 a month for group formats and $100 to $300 for 1-on-1, depending on where the teachers are based. Below that you are usually buying software, not teaching; far above it you are usually paying for US instructor wages or brand, not more depth.

Are coding apps worth it instead of classes?

As a first taste for ages 5 to 7, yes. As the plan for actually learning to build, rarely: apps cannot explain, correct, or hold a child accountable, which is why most app subscriptions go quiet by month two. The honest budget question is whether ~$15 a month of software beats $40 a month of live taught hours.

Why is Modern Age Coders so much less than Juni for 1-on-1?

Geography, not quality. Juni pays US instructor wages, so four sessions cost $275+. Our mentors teach globally from a different wage economy, so eight dedicated hours cost $100. The hour itself, deep, interactive, project-first, is the premium product in both cases. One free demo class lets you judge whose hour teaches more.

What hidden costs should I watch for?

Enrollment and registration fees, long packs sold upfront (Rs 29,500-style bundles in India, $500+ curricula in the US), per-subject pricing that doubles when maths is added, and marketplace prices that creep per booking. We publish flat monthly prices with no fees; most serious providers publish theirs too, so always find the number before the trial call.
