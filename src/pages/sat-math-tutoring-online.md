## The problem we solve

More practice tests don't raise a score — fixing the right problem does. A raw practice
test tells you what you got wrong, not why. Every miss is one of three different problems: a
knowledge gap, a careless slip, or running out of time, and each needs a different fix. The
Digital SAT rewards a small set of algebra and data ideas used quickly and cleanly, so we
diagnose where points leak, fix the algebra underneath, then train speed and trap-spotting
on the exact question types the test repeats.

## A real worked example: a "hard" systems question, read not ground

"A line passes through (0, 3) and is parallel to 6x + 2y = 10. Find its equation."
Parallel means same slope:

- 6x + 2y = 10 → y = −3x + 5 (slope −3)
- same slope −3, passes through (0, 3) → y-intercept 3
- answer: **y = −3x + 3**

No system, no calculator gymnastics — about fifteen seconds once you know "parallel" means
"copy the slope." Students stop fighting the algebra and start reading what the question
asks, which is faster and more accurate at once.

## Why a coding school teaches SAT math

Timed test-taking is debugging under a clock: **recognise the pattern** (the SAT reuses
question types), **choose the efficient path** (like picking the right algorithm), and
**verify before moving on** (a sanity check catches careless slips). Our prep tends to make
students faster and calmer.
