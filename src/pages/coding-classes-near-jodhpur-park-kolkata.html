Online vs offline

An honest comparison for Jodhpur Park families

Where live online wins

  • No commute through the Jodhpur Park signal or Lake Mall traffic — 75–90 minutes saved every class day.
  • Small live batches of 6–8 mean more teacher attention than a crowded Gariahat coaching room.
  • Your child learns on the same computer they will code on, building real screen-and-keyboard fluency.
  • Access to full-time software engineers and maths graduates, not limited to whoever teaches locally.
  • Every class is recordable for revision, and parents can see exactly what was taught.
  • No evening safety worry or winter return through the Dhakuria-bridge jam.
  • Specialised tracks — AI/ML, MERN, DSA, Olympiad maths — that no single neighbourhood centre offers.

When offline still wins

  • Some very young children focus better with a teacher physically beside them at first.
  • Families without reliable broadband or a dedicated quiet corner may struggle with live online.
  • Pure pen-and-paper exam drilling can feel more natural in a physical room for some learners.
  • A child who genuinely thrives on in-person peer energy may prefer a classroom buzz.

For most Jodhpur Park families, live online wins on attention, expertise and saved hours — and a free demo lets you judge the fit before deciding.
