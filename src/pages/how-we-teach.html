The method

How a class actually feels

Four things shape every session — and they work exactly the same whether the screen shows code or a coordinate plane.

01

We start with a spark, not a syllabus

Curiosity first. We open with a question or a tiny win so the student leans in before any "theory" arrives.

Code"Want to make the screen rain emojis?" — then we sneak in the loop.
Maths"Why does every multiple of 3 hide a secret?" — then we meet divisibility.
02

Learning by building, not memorising

Hands on the keyboard, pen on the paper. Every concept becomes something the student makes, not just hears.

CodeThey ship a working mini-game in the same lesson they learn the idea.
MathsThey derive the rule themselves before they ever see it written down.
03

One concept, fully understood

We'd rather go deep on one idea than skim five. We don't move on until it genuinely clicks.

CodeWe trace a loop line-by-line until the student can predict every step.
MathsWe show the same problem three ways until the "aha" lands.
04

Encouragement over correction

Mistakes are data, not failures. Our mentors celebrate the attempt and guide gently to the fix.

Code"Great bug! Let's read what the computer is trying to tell us."
Maths"Your thinking was right — let's find where the number slipped."
