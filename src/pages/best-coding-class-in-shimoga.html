Will coding help with KCET, engineering entrances and board exams?

+

Yes. KCET, engineering exams and Olympiads all reward computational thinking — the decomposition and problem-solving that coding builds every single class. It also lines up directly with CBSE, ICSE/ISC and Karnataka State Board (KSEAB) Computer Science, Informatics Practices and the NEP-2020 AI modules, and a clickable portfolio of built work strengthens any application.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Why learn coding from Shivamogga instead of moving to Bengaluru?

+

For generations, distance from the metros pushed Malnad talent to migrate for tech work. Online learning removes that compulsion: a Shivamogga student learns from the same calibre of instructor a city student gets, and remote and freelance hiring then lets them build and earn for teams in Bengaluru or anywhere on earth — from the hills. Our college track (200+ DSA problems, mock interviews, a MERN build, an AI/Data-Science portfolio and AI-agent skills) is built for exactly that path.

What equipment does my child need?

+

A laptop or desktop and a stable internet connection (broadband or 4G/fibre works well across Shivamogga). A computer rather than a tablet is best for hands-on practice. Minimum: Windows 10 / macOS, 4GB RAM. All the software we use is free, and our team helps with the setup before the first class.

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Shivamogga children juggle school, tuition and activities, so we work around your schedule. Each class is an hour, twice a week, with timezone-friendly slots for families settled outside India.

Start with the free demo and judge the teaching before paying anything. If your first paid week still doesn't feel right for your child, we refund it in full and we part on good terms — we would rather keep a family that genuinely wants to be here than hold on to one that doesn't.
