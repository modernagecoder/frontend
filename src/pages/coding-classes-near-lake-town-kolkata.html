Why it works

Premium, live, and genuinely small-batch

Modern Age Coders is a premium, high-quality live online coding and maths school — every lesson near Lake Town is taught in real time by a full-time engineer or maths graduate in a small batch, not delivered as a pre-recorded video you watch alone. Genuinely live, never pre-recorded

Every single class is taught live, in real time, by a tutor who can see your child's screen and respond the moment they get stuck. There are no canned video lectures and no chatbots standing in for a teacher. Group batches are capped at six to eight, mini batches at three to four, and 1-on-1 means exactly one child. Taught by full-time engineers and maths graduates

Your child is taught by working professional engineers and mathematics graduates who teach full-time — not part-time college students moonlighting between exams. Because they teach all day, they know precisely where learners trip up in Python loops or BlueJ practicals, and how to fix it fast.

Project-based, not theory-only

Children build real, working things — games, web apps, Python programs and AI models — instead of merely copying notes. A concept is only learned when it has been used to build something, so every track ends with a portfolio piece a child can actually show and explain.

Board-aware from day one

We map each child to their own board: ICSE students get Java and BlueJ practicals; CBSE students get Python and Informatics Practices; WBBSE and IGCSE learners are supported too. For Class 11-12, the full ICSE Java & CBSE Python board prep course aligns directly with the exam.

An honest comparison

Where live online wins — and where it doesn't

Where live online wins

  • No commute through the Lake Town crossing or Patipukur underpass — roughly 80-90 minutes saved every class day.
  • Your child learns from the safety of home, with no late-evening auto changes at VIP Road.
  • Access to full-time engineer and maths-graduate tutors, not just whoever the nearest centre could hire.
  • Screen-share teaching means the tutor sees the exact line of code that broke, in real time.
  • Small batches of 6-8, 3-4 or 1-on-1 — genuinely individual attention, not a 30-seat hall.
  • Easy make-ups: miss a class and get a recording of that specific session or a replacement slot.
  • Siblings in the same Bangur Avenue or Dum Dum Park flat can each join their own age-and-level batch without extra travel.

When offline still wins (the honest list)

  • Very young children (under 6) sometimes need a parent physically beside them at first.
  • Families who specifically want a neighbourhood social space for their child to sit in.
  • Homes with genuinely unreliable internet, where a stable connection cannot be arranged.
  • Learners who simply concentrate better with a tutor physically in the room.

For most Lake Town families the maths is simple — same premium teaching, smaller batches, zero commute. If you are unsure, a free demo is the fastest way to judge it for your own child.
