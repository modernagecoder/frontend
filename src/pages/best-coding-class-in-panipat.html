Why Every Child in Panipat Should Learn to Code

Panipat's prosperity was built by families who learned a craft, scaled it and sold it to the world. Coding is the same story for the next generation — a skill that turns a young mind into a maker who can reach a global market from home.

1

Sharper Problem-Solving

Coding trains a child to break a big, messy problem into clean, ordered steps — the same discipline that lifts marks in maths and science and steadies them in a tough board exam.

2

Build From Panipat, Sell Anywhere

Panipat already exports worldwide. Code travels even lighter than cargo — a young coder can take on clients in Delhi, Dubai or the US from a bedroom on Kabri Road, with no need to migrate to a metro.

3

A Maker's Mindset

A weaver's child grows up watching things get made. Coding extends that instinct to the screen — your child stops only consuming apps and games and starts building them, turning idle hours into something they own.

4

NEP 2020 & the School Syllabus

Coding and AI now sit inside the CBSE, ICSE and Haryana Board curricula from the middle years. Children who start early treat school computer lessons as easy revision rather than something brand new.

5

Quiet Help in Entrance Exams

JEE, NTSE and the maths Olympiads all reward the kind of step-by-step computational thinking coding builds week after week. It is rarely the headline reason parents enrol — but it pays off in the exam hall.

6

Work You Can Actually Show

A finished game, a live website, a working app — these are things a child can hand to an interviewer or a college panel and say "I made this." Far more convincing than one more number on a marksheet.
