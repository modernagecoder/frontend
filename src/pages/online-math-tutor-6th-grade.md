# Online math tutor for 6th grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 6 tutor makes ratios visual with tables before cross-multiplication, builds negative numbers on the number line so -7 vs -3 never becomes a coin flip, and turns variables into numbers-in-waiting instead of mysterious letters.

## Why grade 6 feels like a different subject

Arithmetic answered "how many?"; grade 6 asks "how related?" Three new kinds of thinking arrive at once, relational (ratios), directional (negatives), abstract (variables), exactly when the middle-school transition taxes attention. Elementary debts get called in too: the curriculum assumes fractions, decimals and order of operations are finished business.

## Signs a 6th grader needs help

Ratios solved by guess-multiplication; -7 "bigger" than -3; variables cause fog; fraction division is pure flip-and-multiply; every percent problem gets the same move; grade 5 gaps surfacing; transition overload; "when will I use this?"; first B ever, confidence down two letters.

## The complete grade 6 map (Common Core aligned)

- Ratios and Proportional Relationships: ratio language, unit rates, ratio tables and double number lines, percent as rate per 100, conversion by ratio.
- The Number System: fraction divided by fraction, multi-digit fluency, GCF/LCM and distributive rewriting, negatives on the line, absolute value, four-quadrant coordinates.
- Expressions and Equations: whole-number exponents, writing expressions both directions, equivalent expressions, one-step equations and inequalities, dependent/independent variables.
- Geometry: areas by composing/decomposing, volume with fractional edges, polygons on coordinates, surface area from nets.
- Statistics: statistical questions, mean vs median chosen with reason, dot plots, histograms, box plots.
