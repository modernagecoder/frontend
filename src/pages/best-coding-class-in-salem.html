Writing code trains a child to take a tangled problem apart and rebuild it as a clear sequence of steps — exactly the thinking that wins marks in Salem's maths-and-science-heavy classrooms.

2

Salem sends thousands of students into engineering each year. The child who already writes Python before college walks into first year confident, not catching up.

3

From Consumer to Creator

The phone stops being only a place to watch and scroll, and becomes a workshop. Your child starts shipping the apps and games they used to just play — that flip changes everything.

5

An Edge in TNEA, JEE & NEET

The competitive exams Salem families work towards reward computational, logical thinking. Steady coding practice builds that reasoning quietly, week after week — long before the exam pressure arrives.

6

A Portfolio, Not Just a Marksheet

A working game, a live website, a small app — these become real evidence of ability for school showcases and college forms, and they outlast any single set of board marks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Real questions from Salem parents about our coding classes. Can't find yours? Just message us.

Which is the best coding class in Salem for kids?

+

Modern Age Coders runs Salem's top-rated live online coding classes for kids aged 8–12. Children start with Scratch (games, animation, logic) and move to Python, in small batches of 5–8, taught by industry instructors. We serve every locality — Fairlands, Hasthampatti, Five Roads, Alagapuram, Ammapet, Suramangalam, Shevapet, Gugai and Kondalampatti.

Are the classes online or offline, and which Salem areas do you cover?

+

All classes are live and online, so we cover every part of Salem equally — Fairlands, Hasthampatti, Five Roads, Alagapuram, Ammapet, Suramangalam, Shevapet, Gugai, Kondalampatti, the Junction and bus-stand belt, plus the SAIL township and the colleges along Omalur Road. Your child joins the same expert teacher from home — no scooter ride across the Junction traffic. Sessions are fully interactive with live coding, screen-sharing and recordings.

How much do coding classes cost in Salem?

+

Group classes (5–8 students) start at ₹1,499/month for 2 live classes a week. Mini Batch (3–4 students) is ₹2,499/month, and personalised 1-on-1 mentoring is ₹4,999/month. Every new student gets a free demo class first, and there are no hidden charges.

Will coding help with TNEA, JEE and the board exams?

+

Yes. Salem is an engineering-college town where families chase TNEA, JEE and NEET seats. Coding strengthens the exact logical reasoning, decomposition and problem-solving that those exams reward, and it directly supports CBSE and Tamil Nadu State board Computer Science plus the new NEP-2020 AI modules. Our Python and Java tracks line up with what's taught at Vidhya Mandir, Holy Angels, St Paul's and Cluny.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Do you prepare Salem college students for placements?

+

Yes — our college track is placement-focused: 200+ DSA problems, mock interviews, a MERN full-stack build and an AI/Data-Science portfolio. It's designed for students from Government College of Engineering Salem, Sona College of Technology, VMKV and Periyar University who are targeting product companies, service-firm campus drives and the Chennai/Coimbatore IT corridor.

What equipment does my child need?

+

Just a laptop or desktop and a stable internet connection — both common in Salem households now. A computer (rather than a tablet) is best for hands-on practice. Minimum: Windows 10 / macOS, 4GB RAM, broadband. All the software we use is free, and our team helps with setup before the first class.

What are the class timings? Do you have weekend batches?

+

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Salem kids juggle school, tuitions and activities, so we work around your schedule. Classes are an hour long, twice a week.

What if my child doesn't enjoy it — is there a refund?

+

The free demo lets you experience our teaching before committing. If after the first paid week your child isn't satisfied, we offer a full refund — no questions asked. We would rather a child stays because the classes earn it, not because a fee has been collected.

Do you run summer & winter coding camps in Salem?

+

Yes — intensive camps run during the summer break (May–June) and winter break (Dec–Jan), where a child can finish a full course in 4–6 weeks. They're a favourite among Salem parents who want a productive, screen-positive holiday. Ask us for upcoming dates and early-bird offers.
