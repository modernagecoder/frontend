# Online math tutor for 4th grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 4 tutor rebuilds place value deeply enough that multi-digit multiplication and long division make sense, teaches fractions as numbers on the number line before fraction arithmetic, and stays with your child as the same mentor every class.

## Why grade 4 breaks memorizers

A child can fake grade 3 with a good memory; grade 4 ends the act. Multi-digit multiplication has too many steps to memorize blind, long division punishes every place-value gap, and fraction arithmetic is meaningless without the number-line picture. Struggles almost always trace to two or three specific gaps that a diagnostic finds and a mentor rebuilds.

## Signs a 4th grader needs help

Multiplication facts not automatic; long division layouts are random; remainders ignored or misused; unlike-denominator fractions cause shutdown; 0.47 read as "point four seven"; multi-step problems collapse; homework time doubled; confidence dipped after the first big test; hides their work.

## How we teach

Area models before algorithms (23 x 14 as a rectangle in four regions, the standard algorithm as its shorthand); division as repeated reasoning; fractions kept on the number line; estimation before every computation; live and interactive the whole hour; one mentor, compounding.

## The complete grade 4 map (Common Core aligned)

- Operations and Algebraic Thinking: multiplicative comparison, multi-step word problems with remainder interpretation, factors and multiples, primes, patterns.
- Base Ten: place value to 1,000,000, rounding, multi-digit multiplication (area model, partial products, standard algorithm), division with remainders, fluent multi-digit addition and subtraction.
- Fractions: equivalence, comparing unlike fractions by reasoning, adding and subtracting like denominators, multiplying a fraction by a whole number, decimal notation for tenths and hundredths.
- Measurement and Data: unit conversion, area and perimeter formulas, angles measured in degrees, line plots with fractions.
- Geometry: lines, rays, angle types, classifying shapes by properties, line symmetry.

## School-year pacing

Aug-Oct place value and multi-digit add/subtract; Oct-Dec multi-digit multiplication and factors; Dec-Feb long division and multi-step problems; Feb-Apr fractions and decimals (the high-stakes window); Apr-May measurement, angles; May-Jun review and state tests.
