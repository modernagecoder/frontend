Why Kollam is ready for coders

A Port City That Has Always Looked Outward

Kollam — ancient Quilon — has traded across oceans since before the printing press, and today it pairs that worldly instinct with some of India's highest literacy. That mix of curiosity, schooling and global links is exactly the soil coding grows in.

Cashew capital

India's cashew-processing hub

For generations Kollam has shelled, roasted and exported cashew to the world. A trading town raises children who think in terms of value created, not just consumed.

Ashtamudi

a port on the backwaters

Set on the second-largest lake in Kerala, the historic harbour of Quilon connected the Malabar coast to Arabia and China. The world has always arrived here.

TKM legacy

a proud engineering town

TKM College of Engineering, among Kerala's oldest and most respected, has sent Kollam students into tech and abroad for decades. The ambition is already in the water.

NRI & Gulf

digital, globally-linked families

Strong Gulf and overseas ties mean many Kollam households are highly digital and education-first — and they understand, first-hand, that skills travel anywhere.

Context: Kollam's cashew, coir and seafood trades, the Ashtamudi backwaters, TKM's engineering legacy and Kerala's high literacy. We teach the future-ready skills this education-first city values.

Why Every Child in Kollam Should Learn to Code

A high-literacy, globally-connected town gives a child every reason to learn the language of the future. In Kollam, coding is the one skill that lets a young mind in a coastal town build for — and earn from — the whole world.

1

Sharper Problem-Solving

Coding teaches a child to break a big problem into clean, ordered steps — the very habit that lifts maths and science marks and gives an edge in KEAM and NEET aptitude, where Kerala's competition is fierce.

2

A Career With No Postcode

A coder in Kollam can work for a company in Bengaluru, Dubai or San Francisco without moving an inch. For a town that already sends its talent across the Gulf, that is the most natural advantage in the world.

3

From Consumer to Creator

A cashew town built its name by adding value to what it touched. Coding does the same for a child's screen time — the phone stops being a thing they scroll and becomes a workshop where they make games, apps and ideas of their own.

4

NEP 2020 & the School Syllabus

Coding and AI now sit inside the CBSE and Kerala State board curriculum from the middle years. Schools like Infant Jesus, TKM and Bishop Jerome are bringing it in — and early starters stay comfortably ahead.

5

An Edge in KEAM, NEET & Beyond

KEAM, JEE, NEET and Olympiads increasingly reward computational thinking. Regular coding practice builds that reasoning quietly over time — a real advantage in a town that takes its entrance exams seriously.

6

A Portfolio, Not Just a Report Card

Real games, websites and apps become living proof of skill for school exhibitions and college applications — and far more memorable to a TKM admissions panel or an overseas recruiter than one more number on a marksheet.

Will coding help with KEAM, NEET and the Kerala board exams?

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Do you prepare Kollam college students for placements?

+

Yes — our college track is placement-focused: 200+ DSA problems, mock interviews, a MERN full-stack build and an AI/Data-Science portfolio. It's designed for students from TKM College of Engineering, Fatima Mata National College and Amrita Amritapuri who are targeting product companies, the Technopark/Infopark corridor, off-campus offers and remote roles abroad through Kollam's strong NRI networks.

What equipment does my child need?

+

Just a laptop or desktop and a stable internet connection — both easy to find in Kollam, where home broadband is widespread. A computer (rather than a tablet) is best for hands-on practice. Minimum: Windows 10 / macOS, 4GB RAM, broadband. All the software we use is free, and our team helps with setup before the first class.

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Kollam children juggle school, tuition and activities, so we build the timetable around yours. Classes are an hour long, twice a week.
