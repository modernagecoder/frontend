## A real worked example: why a system's solution is where the lines cross

Each linear equation is a line; two equations are two lines; the point satisfying both is
where they cross:

- y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 4 → 2x + 1 = −x + 4 → x = 1, y = 3 → **(1, 3)**

"Solving a system" and "where do the lines intersect" are the same question — and a student
sees why parallel lines give no solution and identical lines give infinitely many. One
picture connects homework algebra, Digital SAT graph questions and AP systems.
