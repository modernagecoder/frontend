Built for the Golpark neighbourhood

Golpark is the cultural heart of South Kolkata — the point where Gariahat Road meets Rashbehari Avenue, anchored by the Ramakrishna Mission Institute of Culture. The families here are old-Calcutta Bengali households, academically exacting and deeply rooted in classical music, recitation and literature. Many flats are inherited and have held three generations; in these homes, education sits above almost everything else, and a coding class is judged the way a raga is judged — on depth, not decoration.

Parents in this neighbourhood are quiet, well-read and slow to commit. They attend a demo, sit at the back, observe carefully, and join only once they are convinced of substance. We built our classes for exactly this scrutiny: small live batches, real engineers, a visible syllabus, and a child who can explain what they built. There is no hard sell here — only a teacher and a screen full of working code that earns its place between the tabla lesson and the dinner table.

The practical truth is that an evening commute defeats the routine. The crawl from Golpark down Southern Avenue, the wait through Gariahat crossing, the auto-change at the Rashbehari junction — together they can swallow ninety minutes on a class day. Live online classes erase all of it. A child finishes a 4 PM Carnatic or recitation lesson at home, opens the laptop, and is in a live Python class by 5 PM with the same teacher every week.
