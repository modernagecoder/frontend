The neighbourhood

Premium teaching, finally where the child already is

Ramrajatala is one of the oldest, most tightly-packed corners of southern Howrah — a Bengali working-class belt of mill-and-railway families, small traders and shopkeepers, grown up around its century-old Ram temple and the little Ramrajatala station on the Howrah–Kharagpur line, barely 6 km from Howrah Station. The annual Rama puja still draws the whole locality. It is a place of sincere, value-conscious, tuition-driven education — and very few in-locality options for coding or higher mathematics.

That gap is exactly what we close. For decades, an ambitious Ramrajatala child who wanted serious coding had to travel: an auto to Santragachi, a crowded local towards Howrah, then the bridge to a Kolkata coaching centre — ninety minutes each way, every class day, through the choked level-crossing and the evening Andul Road snarl. For a daily-wage or shopkeeping family, that cost of time and fare quietly priced good teaching out of reach.

Live online removes the travel entirely and keeps the quality. The same full-time engineer who teaches a Salt Lake teenager teaches a child off Belilious Road or near Shankar Math, in the same small batch, at ₹1,499/month. New apartment families pushing down the Andul Road and Kona Expressway corridor get it too. This is not a discount product — it is the same premium class, delivered where the child already is.

Where live online wins — and where it doesn't

Where live online wins

  • Zero commute — no Ramrajatala level-crossing, no Andul Road or GT Road traffic, no auto-swap at Santragachi, no bridge to Kolkata; about 90 minutes saved each class day.
  • Access to full-time engineers and maths graduates a small Howrah locality could never staff offline.
  • Small live batches of 6–8 with real-time screen sharing and instant doubt-clearing.
  • The same premium teacher and curriculum as a Salt Lake child — true equity, not a watered-down local version.
  • Safer for kids and girls — no evening travel through crowded crossings; learning happens at home.
  • Recorded revision support and shared notes after each live class for missed concepts.
  • Lower total cost — no fares, no fuel, no time lost; from ₹1,499/month.

When offline still wins (honest)

  • A child who genuinely cannot sit at a screen unsupervised may do better with a physical tutor at first.
  • Very young children (under 6) often need a parent or adult beside them for the first few sessions.
  • Homes with no reliable laptop or stable internet will find live class harder — though a basic device is enough.
  • Some families simply prefer face-to-face contact, and that is a fair personal choice.

For almost every Ramrajatala family, live online delivers a better teacher, a smaller batch and a real project portfolio — without the daily commute or the cost of crossing the bridge.
