## What drives the price

1. Real estate and franchise overheads (centers).
2. The tutor labour market: US tutors price against US wages; global live-online schools pay excellent teachers well in their own economies.
3. Format efficiency: 1-on-1 vs group; scheduled vs ad-hoc.
4. What you actually buy: drilling, floor time, and deep interactive teaching all get called "tutoring".
5. Fees around the fee: enrollment, registration, assessments, lock-ins.
6. Grade level: elementary $23-75/hr, high school $30-100/hr.

## FAQ highlights

**How much per hour?** $25-80 covers most competent private US tutors; under $20 is usually a student tutor or a global online school; above $80 buys specialist branding.

**Monthly program or hourly tutor?** Monthly for sustained help (rhythm + lower per-hour cost); hourly for short-term targeted gaps.

**Why are live online schools less expensive?** No real estate, no franchise fees, globally hired teachers. Structural difference, not a quality discount.
