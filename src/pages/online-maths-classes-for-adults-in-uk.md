## A real worked example: why "the average" can mislead

There are two everyday averages. The mean shares everything out; the median is the middle
value. A few big numbers pull the mean up while the median stays put:

- salaries: £20k, £22k, £24k, £26k, £180k
- mean = £54.4k (pulled up by one big earner)
- median = £24k (what a typical person earns)

The mean is more than double the median; the median is the honest picture of "typical".
Statistics in the news, at work and on a payslip stop being intimidating once you can
question them — the foundation under GCSE statistics and the data skills jobs expect.
