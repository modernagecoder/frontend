# Calculus tutoring online: three ideas, taught like ideas

**The 20-second answer:** a good calculus tutor teaches the limit as approach, the derivative as instantaneous rate, and the integral as accumulated change before any rule tables; repairs the precalculus the course silently assumes (composition, unit circle); and meets your student's actual course.

## Why calculus defeats prepared students

The computations are mostly easier than Algebra 2; the course runs on three deep ideas most classrooms hope arrive by osmosis. A student with forty derivative rules but no sense of what a derivative IS freezes on related rates, because those ask about the idea. Second saboteur: precalculus debt, weak composition makes the chain rule a lottery; most "calculus errors" on exams die in the algebra.

## The complete first-year map

Limits and continuity; the derivative from the shrinking-interval story; rules with the chain rule as composition unwrapping; implicit and inverse differentiation; applications (related rates from pictures, optimization, curve sketching via f' and f''); the definite integral as Riemann accumulation; the Fundamental Theorem earned as a discovery; substitution as the chain rule reversed (+ parts and partial fractions for BC/college); areas, volumes, average value; separable differential equations and slope fields.

## Signature teaching moves

The derivative discovered via average speeds over shrinking intervals (one table, both limit and derivative met as one idea); the chain rule as onion layers; the FTC as the punchline it is; calculus in code (numerical derivatives, Riemann loops in Python) for students who want it.
