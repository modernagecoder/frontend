Make the hour count

## What to watch for during the trial: a parent's checklist.

Use the trial to evaluate us the way we evaluate fit. Watch for these six things, in any online class, from anyone:

### Who talks more?

In a good class the child talks, builds and explains more than the teacher. Lecture-heavy hours are a warning sign anywhere.

### Are questions asked, or answers given?

Watch whether the mentor asks "what do you think happens?" before showing. That single habit predicts everything.

### Does the level adjust live?

Too easy should get harder within minutes; too hard should get gentler. Static scripts cannot do this; real teachers do.

### How are mistakes treated?

Errors should become the best moments of the hour, examined with curiosity, never rushed past or shamed.

### Is your child lighter afterward?

The walk-away test: does your child leave the hour more confident and talkative about the subject than they entered?

### Was the read honest?

A placement that flatters everything is a sales script. Ours will name a real starting point and a real gap if one exists.

## Everything families ask about the free trial.

Is the trial really free? What is the catch?

Really free, and the catch is openly commercial: we believe one real hour of our teaching sells better than any advertisement, so we give the hour away. About the demo we ask only one thing, that a parent be reachable afterward for the placement conversation.

Do you need my card details?

No, at no point in the trial process. Payment details only enter the picture if and when you choose to enroll, and even then billing is monthly with no contract.

Coding or maths, which should we trial?

Whichever your child needs or wants more; many families trial both (two children, or the same child across two slots). If you are unsure, say so on the callback and the coordinator will help you choose, the wrong answer does not exist here.

What ages do you take?

Ages 6 to 65, genuinely: kids and teens across coding and maths, college students, and working professionals. The trial format is the same at every age, one real hour, honest read.

Is the trial 1-on-1 or group?

1-on-1, always, even if you later choose the small-group price. Placement needs the mentor's full attention on one child.

What equipment do we need?

A computer or laptop with a browser and stable internet. That covers every trial; anything course-specific gets confirmed later if you enroll.

What happens after the trial if we say no?

Nothing. No drip campaign, no daily calls. You keep the free lesson and the honest read, and you are welcome back whenever the timing is right.

How fast can the trial happen?

Usually within a day or two of the callback, sometimes same-day if slots align. Booking now means your child could be in a real class this week.
