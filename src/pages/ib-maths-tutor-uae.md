## A real worked example: why logarithms turn multiplication into addition

A logarithm asks "what power gives this number?", and powers turn multiplication into
addition:

- 10² × 10³ = 10⁵ (multiply → add exponents 2 + 3)
- log₁₀(10⁵) = 5 = 2 + 3 = log₁₀(10²) + log₁₀(10³)
- so log(a × b) = log a + log b

The log law is the exponent rule seen from the other direction. Logarithms convert hard
multiplication into easy addition — the foundation of pH, decibels and the AI models
reshaping the world. That depth is what HL and the IA reward.
