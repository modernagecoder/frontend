## A real worked example: compound interest

Interest doesn't add in a straight line — each year compounds on the new balance:

- simple: 5% × 10 years = +50% → AED 10,000 becomes AED 15,000
- compound: 10,000 × 1.05¹⁰ = **AED 16,289** (the extra AED 1,289 is interest on interest)

The gap is small over ten years and enormous over thirty — why compounding matters for
savings and is dangerous on debt. Compounding is repeated multiplication, not repeated
addition; once that's clear, financial decisions become calculable.
