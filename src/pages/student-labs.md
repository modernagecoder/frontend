## Frequently asked questions

What projects can beginners build after learning to code?Beginners can build projects like word counters, image sliders, memory games (like the Simon Game above) and simple calculators. As they progress they move on to weather apps, typing challenges and interactive websites. Students typically build their first project within 2–3 months of starting.How long does it take to build a complete project?Simple projects like a word counter or image slider take 1–2 weeks. Intermediate projects like weather apps or food trackers take 3–4 weeks. Advanced projects like AI chatbots, brain-training games or full-stack platforms can take 2–3 months — with an instructor guiding every step.Do students get certificates for their projects?Yes. Students receive a project completion certificate for every major project they build, which can be added to college applications, LinkedIn and resumes. The best projects are also showcased here on Student Labs.What technologies will students learn to build these projects?Students learn industry-standard technologies including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, React, Node.js, MongoDB, Firebase and AI/ML frameworks. The specific stack depends on the course and project chosen.Can my child's project be featured on Student Labs?Absolutely. Any enrolled student can submit a completed project for review. Projects that show creativity, technical skill and completeness are featured here with the student's name and a live link.What age groups can participate in project-based learning?Project-based learning is for all ages — kids (6–12), teens (13–18), college students and working professionals. Projects are tailored to each level, from Scratch games for young kids to full-stack AI apps for advanced learners.your move
