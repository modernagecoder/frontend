A complete map of coding & maths

The Course Atlas is the companion to our interactive course explorer — one readable page that gathers every Modern Age Coders program in a single catalog. Whether you are a parent comparing options for a six-year-old, a teenager preparing for board exams, a college student working toward placements, or a professional adding AI to your toolkit, you can see the whole landscape here and choose with confidence.

Five tracks, one philosophy

Courses are organised into five tracks — Kids (6–12), Teens (13–17), College & Professionals, Girls in Tech and Mathematics. Each runs on the same idea: people learn to build by building. Classes are live and small, mentors give real feedback, and fast-moving subjects like AI agents and generative AI are treated as living syllabuses that update as the tools change.

What you walk away with

A certificate, yes — but more importantly a portfolio of real projects: games, apps, websites, machine-learning models and trading bots that prove skill far better than any line on a resume. That is the difference between watching tutorials and actually being able to build.

Questions, answered

How many courses are there?
More than seventy live, project-based programs across coding, artificial intelligence, web and app development, data science, cybersecurity and mathematics — organised into five tracks: Kids, Teens, College & Professionals, Girls in Tech, and Mathematics. They are all listed on this page.
What age groups can enrol?
Learners aged 6 to 67. Kids start with visual block coding and Scratch; teens move into Python, web development, AI and board-aligned Computer Science; college students and professionals tackle full-stack development, machine learning, generative AI and DSA. Mathematics runs from elementary through college level.
Are the classes live or recorded?
All classes are live and interactive, in small batches of typically four to eight students, so every learner gets personal attention. Recorded sessions are provided afterwards for revision.
Do I need prior experience?
No. Most courses are designed for complete beginners and start from first principles. Intermediate and advanced tracks exist for those who already have a foundation, and we help you choose the right level — you can also book a trial class first.
Will I get a certificate and a portfolio?
Yes. Every course ends with a certificate of completion, and along the way you build a portfolio of real projects — the work that genuinely impresses schools, colleges and employers.
How is this different from the courses explorer?
This Atlas is a single, readable catalog of every program with thumbnails, tabs and search. The interactive courses explorer offers the same courses with additional filtering. Both link to the same detailed course pages.
With AI changing tech so fast, which course should I choose to stay relevant?
Choose by depth, not just by topic. AI tools are changing every field on this page, so the durable skill is understanding the fundamentals well enough to use those tools wisely and to keep learning as they evolve. For most people that means a strong programming foundation, usually starting with Python, then a track aligned to your goal: AI and machine learning, full-stack development, data science, or a board-aligned course for school. Every course here is live and project-based and teaches the underlying ideas, not just today's tools, so your learning stays relevant as the tools change.
