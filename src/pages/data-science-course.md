## Frequently Asked Questions

What is data science and what does the course cover?+

Data Science is the practice of turning data into insight and predictions. Our course covers Python with NumPy and pandas, data cleaning and wrangling, exploratory data analysis, visualisation with Matplotlib/Seaborn, statistics, SQL basics and an introduction to machine learning — all hands-on with real datasets.

Do I need maths or Python before the data science course?+

No advanced maths is needed. We teach the Python and statistics you need as part of the course. Basic comfort with computers is enough to start; if you already know some Python you'll move faster — or build it with [Python for Data Science](/python-for-data-science). After the free demo we place you at the right level.

What will I build in the data science course?+

You complete real end-to-end projects — analysing and visualising a dataset, cleaning messy data, finding insights, and building a simple predictive model — finishing with a data-science portfolio you can show for jobs, internships or college. See [AI/ML projects](/ai-ml-projects-for-students).

What is the difference between data science, data analytics and machine learning?+

Data analytics focuses on understanding past data and reporting insights; machine learning focuses on building predictive models; data science covers the whole pipeline — collecting, cleaning, analysing and modelling data. Our course gives you the full skill set and links to specialised [analytics](/data-analytics-course) and [ML](/machine-learning-course-for-beginners) tracks.

Are the data science classes live and project-based?+

Yes. Classes are live in small batches with a mentor who reviews your notebooks and code, and every topic is applied to real datasets and projects. All sessions are recorded for revision.
