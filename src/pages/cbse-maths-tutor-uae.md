## A real worked example: why (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²

(a + b)² is the area of a square of side (a + b). Split each side into a and b — the square
breaks into four pieces:

- a×a = a², a×b = ab, b×a = ab, b×b = b²
- total = a² + ab + ab + b² = **a² + 2ab + b²**

The "2ab" is obvious — two identical ab rectangles. A student who sees this never drops the
middle term (the most common CBSE algebra error) and understands (a − b)² and (a + b)(a − b)
the same way.
