A Head Start for the Engineering Dream

For Kumaon families who hope their child will crack an engineering entrance, the structured reasoning those exams reward grows naturally out of regular, hands-on coding practice.

6

Something to Show, Not Just Marks

Real games, sites and apps your child can open and demonstrate. A working project says "I can build" far more convincingly than another number on a Kumaon school marksheet.

Which is the best coding class in Haldwani for kids?

+

Modern Age Coders runs Haldwani's top-rated live online coding classes for kids aged 8–12. Children begin on Scratch — building games, animation and logic — before stepping up to Python, all in small batches of five to eight, with teachers who have actually built software. We reach children across Mukhani, Kathgodam, Nainital Road, Rampur Road, Kaladhungi Road, Tikonia and Damuadhunga, plus the wider Kumaon belt — Lalkuan, Bhimtal, Nainital and Ramnagar.

Are the classes online or offline, and do you cover the hill towns around Haldwani?

+

Every class is live and online, so we cover Haldwani and the whole Kumaon belt equally — Mukhani, Kathgodam, Nainital Road, Rampur Road, Kaladhungi Road, Awas Vikas and Indira Nagar, plus the hill and Terai towns around it: Lalkuan, Bhimtal, Nainital, Almora and Ramnagar. Your child joins the same expert teacher from home, with no coming down to the city and no travel up the hill road. Each lesson is fully two-way — live coding, screen-sharing and a recording saved for later revision.

How much do coding classes cost in Haldwani?

+

Group classes (5–8 students) start at ₹1,499/month for 2 live classes a week. Mini Batch (3–4 students) is ₹2,499/month, and personalised 1-on-1 mentoring is ₹4,999/month. Every new student gets a free demo class first, and there are no hidden charges.

Will coding help with engineering entrances and the CBSE, ICSE & UBSE board exams?

+

Yes. Coding strengthens the exact logical reasoning, decomposition and problem-solving that engineering entrances (JEE) and Olympiads reward, and it directly supports CBSE, ICSE and Uttarakhand Board (UBSE) Computer Science, Informatics Practices and the new NEP-2020 AI modules. Our Python and Java tracks line up with what Haldwani and Kumaon students study across boards from Class 9 onward.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Do you prepare Haldwani & Kumaon college students for placements and remote jobs?

+

Yes — the college track is built end-to-end around placements: 200+ DSA problems, full mock-interview rounds, a complete MERN project and a genuine AI/Data-Science portfolio. It's designed for students of Soban Singh Jeena University, engineering aspirants and serious learners across Kumaon who want to win remote, freelance and product-company roles — and build a tech career from Haldwani without relocating to Dehradun, Delhi or Bengaluru.

What equipment does my child need?

+

A laptop or desktop and a stable internet link — broadband or a decent 4G/5G connection, which now reaches most of Haldwani and the larger Kumaon towns. A computer (not a tablet) works best for hands-on practice; minimum Windows 10 / macOS with 4GB RAM. Every tool we teach with is free to download, and we walk you through the setup before the very first class.

What are the class timings? Do you have weekend batches?

+

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Kumaon children juggle school, tuition and family time, so we fit the class around your week. Each session is an hour long, twice a week.

What if my child doesn't enjoy it — is there a refund?

+

Start with the free demo so you can see the teaching before paying anything. And if the first paid week isn't right for your child, simply tell us and we return the fee in full. We would far rather a child stay because the classes genuinely suit them than out of any sense of obligation.

Do you run summer & winter coding camps in Haldwani?

+

Yes — we run intensive camps across the summer break (May–June) and the winter break (Dec–Jan), in which a child can complete a whole course in just 4–6 weeks. Kumaon families love them for turning a long mountain holiday into real progress. Message us for the next batch dates and early-bird offers.
