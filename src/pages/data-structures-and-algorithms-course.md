## Full curriculum (covered in depth)
- **Module 00 — Foundations:** time & space complexity (Big-O), asymptotic analysis, reading constraints, a framework to attack any problem.
- **Module 01 — Arrays & Strings:** traversals, in-place tricks, prefix sums, two pointers, sliding window.
- **Module 02 — Searching & Sorting:** binary search (and binary search on the answer), merge sort, quick sort, sorting patterns.
- **Module 03 — Hashing:** hash maps & sets, frequency counting, O(n²)→O(n) lookups.
- **Module 04 — Stacks & Queues:** stacks, queues, deques, monotonic stack.
- **Module 05 — Linked Lists:** singly & doubly, reversal, cycle detection, fast/slow pointers.
- **Module 06 — Trees & BSTs:** binary trees, BSTs, all traversals, recursive reasoning.
- **Module 07 — Heaps & Priority Queues:** min/max heaps, heapify, top-K, k-way merge.
- **Module 08 — Tries & advanced strings:** prefix trees, autocomplete, string matching.
- **Module 09 — Graphs:** representations, BFS/DFS, topological sort, Dijkstra, union-find/DSU, MST.
- **Module 10 — Recursion & Backtracking:** subsets, permutations, combinations, N-Queens.
- **Module 11 — Greedy & Dynamic Programming:** greedy proofs; full DP ladder — 1D/2D, knapsack, LIS, DP on grids/strings/trees.
- **Plus:** bit manipulation, maths for DSA, and advanced structures (segment trees, Fenwick/BIT) once the core is solid.

## Our method (how we build your logic)
We never just hand you answers. Every topic starts from the obvious-but-slow idea, then you discover the insight that makes it fast — e.g. Two Sum: brute-force checking every pair is O(n²) and slow; remembering each number in a hash map and looking up `target − num` makes it one clean O(n) pass. That leap from brute force to insight is the logic we train on every topic until it becomes how you think.
