worked examples and pattern-match on exams — which works until the question is phrased

## A real worked example: what a derivative actually is

Before any rules, a derivative is the slope of a curve at a point, found by zooming in
until the curve looks straight — the limit of a secant line:

- average slope: ( f(x+h) − f(x) ) / h
- let h → 0: f′(x) = lim (f(x+h) − f(x)) / h
- for f(x) = x²: ((x+h)² − x²)/h = 2x + h → **2x**

The power rule is just what this limit always produces. Seen this way, related rates,
optimization and linear approximation become the same idea in different clothes — the
difference between scraping a C and owning the course.

## Why a coding school teaches math

Proof and program are the same discipline: **precise definitions** (like a function
