The CBSE CS Practical Problem No One Talks About

The CBSE Class 12 Computer Science practical exam is worth 30 marks — and it terrifies most students. Why? Because it demands 15 working Python programmes, 5 SQL query sets, CSV file processing, and a Python-MySQL connectivity project, all written from scratch under exam conditions. School computer labs offer theory and occasionally supervised typing — not the independent problem-solving that the practical exam actually tests. Our dedicated CBSE CS Practical Bootcamp is built around exactly this gap. Every BHS student who has gone through it walks into their Class 12 CS practical not just confident — but ahead of the question paper. Beyond exams, from 2026 CBSE has shifted 50% of theory exam weightage to Competency-Based Questions — meaning application and analysis, not rote answers. Coding teaches exactly that skill.

When your son combines solid coding skills with deep mathematical reasoning, he doesn't just aim for IIT — he gets there. Families from Bhowanipore, Ballygunge, Alipore, and Park Circus trust us because we understand the BHS culture of ambition.

This is our most popular course for BHS Class 12 students. The CBSE CS practical requires 15 Python programmes, 5 SQL query sets, CSV file processing, and a Python-MySQL connectivity project. Our dedicated CBSE CS Practical Bootcamp covers every single requirement. Students who go through it consistently score 27–30/30 in their practicals.

All classes are live online — no commute from Elgin, Bhowanipore, Ballygunge, or Alipore. We offer weekday evening batches (4 PM – 8 PM) and full weekend batches (Saturday & Sunday, 9 AM – 7 PM). Does coding help BHS students score better in CBSE Maths?+
Consistently, yes. Coding teaches structured problem-solving — the exact skill CBSE's new Competency-Based Question format tests. BHS students who learn coding alongside maths consistently report improved scores across both subjects, stronger analytical reasoning, and better performance in the application-based questions that now make up 50% of CBSE board exams.
