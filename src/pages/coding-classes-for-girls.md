## STEM Education for Girls in India: Closing the Gender Gap

The numbers tell the story. But every girl who learns to code changes the equation.

Only 26% of India's tech workforce is women. Fewer than 15% of leadership roles in Indian tech companies are held by women. And the gap starts early: fewer girls choose science and maths in school, which means fewer women in engineering and computer science programs later.

The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 recognizes this gap and emphasizes STEM education for all genders from an early age. Research consistently shows that when girls are exposed to coding and maths before age 12, they are significantly more likely to pursue STEM careers. The key is early access, the right mentors, and a supportive environment.

At Modern Age Coders, we are doing our part. Our girls-only coding programs (Princess Coders, Code Queens, and She Coders Elite) provide a safe, encouraging space where girls build real projects, develop confidence, and discover that coding and maths are not "boys' subjects." They are for everyone.

100% of our Code Queens batch graduates go on to pursue STEM, CS, or tech-adjacent careers. That is the kind of impact early STEM education can have. [Read our full guide on STEM education for girls in India](/blog/stem-education-for-girls-india).
