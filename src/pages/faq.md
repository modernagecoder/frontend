## Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our courses, pricing, and enrollment

### What is Modern Age Coders?

+

Modern Age Coders is a platform dedicated to teaching Coding and Maths to young minds aged 6—18 and college students aged 18–21. We also collaborate with companies to help build their systems and automation tools, offering practical exposure to real-world projects.

### Do I need any prior coding experience?

+

No prior experience is necessary for our beginner courses! We start from the absolute basics. For advanced courses, we recommend completing our foundational programs first.

### How do online classes work?

+

Our classes combine live instruction with interactive coding environments. Students attend scheduled virtual sessions, complete hands-on projects, and have access to recordings and resources.

### What equipment does my child need?

+

Students need a computer (Windows, Mac, or Chromebook) with a reliable internet connection. Most of our courses run in the browser, so no special software installation is required.

### What age groups are your courses for?

+

Our courses are designed for students aged 6-21, with different difficulty levels tailored to specific age groups. We have beginner courses for younger students and advanced material for teens and college students.

### What courses do you offer?

+

We offer a range of Coding and Maths courses tailored to different age groups and skill levels — from beginners to advanced. Our curriculum includes programming, computational thinking, and maths modules. Check our courses page for the full list.

### Where are your classes conducted?

+

All classes are conducted online to ensure accessibility and convenience for students everywhere, enabling learners to attend from any location.

### What is the duration of each course?

+

Course durations vary by level and subject: basic courses typically run 3–6 months, while advanced specializations can run 6–9 months. Custom programs may have flexible timelines.

### What programming languages do you teach?

+

We teach Python, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Java, C++, and block-based platforms like Scratch. Our courses pair coding with mathematical concepts where appropriate.

### Do you prepare students for competitive exams?

+

Yes, we prepare students for coding competitions and olympiads — combining algorithmic practice with problem-solving techniques to strengthen their competitive performance.

### Are the courses project-based?

+

Yes — every course includes hands-on projects to ensure practical understanding. Projects are designed to simulate real-world scenarios and give students practical exposure.

### Are classes live or pre-recorded?

+

Classes are conducted live, with recordings available for revision. Live sessions cover interactive coding demos and problem walkthroughs.

### What is the batch size?

+

We maintain small batches to ensure personalized attention for each student. Small groups are ideal for hands-on help and doubt-clearing.

### How can I enroll my child?

+

You can contact us through our contact page or call us directly. Our enrollment process will help identify the right batch for your child.

### What is the fee structure?

+

Fees vary depending on the course, level, and duration. Visit our pricing page for detailed information about all our plans.

### Do you offer any free trial classes?

+

Yes, we offer one free trial class so students and parents can evaluate our teaching style. Contact us to schedule your trial session.

### Is there an option to pay fees in installments?

+

Yes, we provide flexible payment options including installments for many courses. Contact us to discuss payment plans.

### What if I miss a class?

+

Recorded sessions are available for all classes so you can catch up. Recordings include coding demos and explanations.

### Do you provide technical support?

+

Yes, our support team assists with technical issues related to classes and access. We also help with setup for tools used in lessons.

### Do you offer certificates after completion?

+

Yes, every student receives a certificate upon completing their course. The certificate mentions the skills learned during the program.

### Do you help with internships or real-world projects?

+

Yes, advanced students get opportunities to work on real projects. These projects allow students to apply their skills in building actual systems.

### Can parents track their child's progress?

+

Yes, parents receive periodic progress reports. Teachers also share feedback during review sessions to help parents stay updated.

### Do you conduct assessments or tests?

+

Regular assessments are part of our learning process. Students are tested to strengthen their analytical approach using real-world logic problems.

### What if my child struggles with a topic?

+

We provide additional doubt-clearing sessions and one-on-one support. Our instructors ensure that no student is left behind.

### Do you teach artificial intelligence or robotics?

+

Yes, advanced batches include modules on AI, Robotics, and Automation. These involve both coding logic and mathematical understanding.

### Can college students join your programs?

+

Yes, our college-level programs focus on practical coding skills, advanced mathematics, and exposure to real projects to prepare students for internships and employment.

### Do you offer career guidance for tech students?

+

Yes, we guide students on choosing their paths in tech. Industry experts share insights on real work and opportunities.

### Do you offer personalized 1:1 mentoring?

+

Yes, we provide personalized mentoring sessions to ensure focused learning. Students can choose 1:1 guidance for advanced topics.

### Can students participate in hackathons or competitions?

+

Yes, we encourage students to join hackathons and coding challenges. Our sessions are designed to improve their analytical skills for such competitions.

### Do you teach web development and app building?

+

Yes! We teach complete web and app development using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. Students learn how to build real products.
