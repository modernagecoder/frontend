## A real worked example: what a p-value is

A p-value answers a conditional question, and the order matters:

- p-value = P(data this extreme | null hypothesis true)
- NOT P(null hypothesis true | data)
- p = 0.03 means "if H₀ were true, data this extreme would occur only 3% of the time" — so we doubt H₀

Assume the coin is fair, then see how rare your run of heads is — the p-value measures the
surprise *given* fairness, never the chance the coin is fair. Get the direction of that
conditional right and every inference conclusion you write becomes correct. This is among the
most heavily tested ideas on the exam.
