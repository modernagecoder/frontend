Why Every Child in Kakinada Should Learn to Code

In a city built on technical work — a deep-water port, petrochemicals and a major engineering university — coding isn't a far-off "future skill". It's the natural next step for a delta child who already lives among engineers and builders.

1

Sharper Problem-Solving

Coding trains a child to break big problems into clean, ordered steps — the exact habit that lifts marks in maths, science and the EAPCET-style reasoning Kakinada classrooms drill.

2

A Genuine Hometown Advantage

JNTU-K, the port industries and an STPI node since 2007 mean technical careers are already part of Kakinada life. Kids who code early simply walk into a world their city understands.

3

From Consumer to Creator

A phone can either entertain a child or teach one to build. Coding flips the screen from something they passively scroll into something they actively make — a shift in identity that outlasts any single course.

4

NEP 2020 & the School Syllabus

Coding and AI now sit inside the CBSE/AP-board curriculum from the middle years. Kakinada schools like Bhashyam, Sri Chaitanya and Aditya already teach it — early starters stay comfortably ahead.

5

An Edge in EAPCET, JEE & Beyond

AP EAPCET, JEE and Olympiads increasingly reward computational thinking. Regular coding practice strengthens that reasoning naturally — a quiet advantage for a delta engineering aspirant.

6

A Portfolio, Not Just a Report Card

Real games, websites and apps become proof of skill for school exhibitions and college applications — far more memorable to admissions panels than another rank on a marksheet.

Group classes (5–8 students) start at ₹1,499/month for 2 live classes a week. Mini Batch (3–4 students) is ₹2,499/month, and personalised 1-on-1 mentoring is ₹4,999/month. Every new student gets a free demo class first, and there are no hidden charges.

Will coding help with EAPCET, JEE and the CBSE/AP board exams?

+

Yes. Kakinada is a deeply engineering-minded city anchored by JNTU-K. Coding strengthens the exact logical reasoning, decomposition and problem-solving that AP EAPCET (EAMCET), JEE and Olympiads reward, and it directly supports CBSE/AP-board Computer Science and the new NEP-2020 AI modules. Our Python and Java tracks line up with what's taught at Bhashyam, Sri Chaitanya, Aditya and Carmel.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Do you prepare JNTU Kakinada & engineering college students for placements?

+

Yes — our college track is placement-focused: 200+ DSA problems, mock interviews, a MERN full-stack build and an AI/Data-Science portfolio. It's designed for students from JNTU Kakinada, Pragati Engineering and Aditya who are targeting TCS, Infosys, Cognizant and product-company roles — including remote jobs, so they don't have to relocate to begin a strong career.

What equipment does my child need?

+

Just a laptop or desktop and a stable internet connection — both common in Kakinada homes now. A computer (rather than a tablet) is best for hands-on practice. Minimum: Windows 10 / macOS, 4GB RAM, broadband. All the software we use is free, and our team helps with setup before the first class.

What are the class timings? Do you have weekend batches?

+

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Kakinada kids juggle school, coaching and activities, so we work around your schedule. Classes are an hour long, twice a week.

What if my child doesn't enjoy it — is there a refund?

+

The free demo lets you judge our teaching before you pay anything. If your child still isn't happy after the first paid week, we return your fee in full — no questions, no friction. We would rather earn a family that wants to stay than hold one that doesn't.

Do you run summer & winter coding camps in Kakinada?

+

Yes — intensive camps run during the summer break (May–June) and winter break (Dec–Jan), where a child can finish a full course in 4–6 weeks. They're a favourite among Kakinada parents who want a productive, screen-positive holiday. Ask us for upcoming dates and early-bird offers.
