Why Every Child in Berhampur Should Learn to Code

A weaver counts every thread before the shuttle moves; a coder counts every step before the program runs. Berhampur's children already grow up around that patient, exacting craft — and that habit of mind is the quiet head start coding rewards.

1

Sharper Problem-Solving

Breaking a problem into clean, ordered steps is the heart of both a fine weave and good code — and the habit that lifts a Berhampur child's maths, science and board-exam reasoning.

2

A Career You Build From Berhampur

Software is the rare high-value career that doesn't demand leaving for Bhubaneswar or Hyderabad. Berhampur's traders always sold beyond the town; remote, freelance and product roles let a young coder do the same.

3

A Maker's Instinct, Not a Spectator's

A child who codes stops asking "what can this app do for me?" and starts asking "how would I build it?" That shift from watching to making is the most valuable thing coding teaches.

4

NEP 2020 & the School Syllabus

Coding and AI now sit inside the CBSE, ICSE and Odisha Board curricula. A Berhampur child who starts early treats school computer science as revision, not a fresh hurdle.

5

An Edge in Entrance Exams

JEE, NEET-adjacent aptitude, NTSE and Olympiads all reward computational thinking. For a Berhampur city that already aims for engineering and MKCG-style medical seats, regular coding quietly sharpens that reasoning.

6

Something to Show, Not Just Marks

Real games, sites and apps for school showcases and college applications — a project says a Berhampur student can build, not only that they can pass.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Around age 8 is a good time to start with visual, block-based coding (Scratch) — early enough to build confidence, late enough to follow instructions independently.
