## Premium live teaching, delivered to your home in Liluah

Every Modern Age Coders class is delivered live online by a full-time engineer or maths graduate to a small batch — there are no pre-recorded lectures, no passive video-watching, and no oversized classrooms. For Liluah families, that means premium, high-quality coding and maths education with the discipline of one-to-few attention, delivered straight to your home without a single trip over the Liluah crossing.

### Live, never recorded

Every session is taught in real time by a teacher who can see your child's screen, answer questions on the spot and adjust pace. Nobody is handed a playlist of videos to watch alone — learning happens live, with a human, every single class.

### Genuinely small batches

We cap groups at 6–8, run mini-batches of 3–4, and offer fully 1-on-1 slots. In a town that respects the precision of the railway workshop, we keep batches small enough that no child can hide at the back or fall a week behind unnoticed.

### Taught by working engineers

Classes are led by full-time engineers and maths graduates, not part-time hobbyists or rotating interns. The same people who write production code and solve real maths teach your child how to think, debug and build — the way Liluah's fitters and supervisors learned their craft on the job.

### Project-based from day one

Children build real, working things — apps, games, web pages and data tools — rather than memorising syntax for a test. A finished project your child can demo to family beats a notebook full of copied code every time.

### Board-aware curriculum

We teach the exact Java/BlueJ programs ICSE Class 9–10 students need and the Python/Informatics Practices that CBSE Class 11–12 students sit for. Coding skill and board marks grow together, mapped to Don Bosco, MCKV, Sudhir Memorial and KV Howrah syllabi.

### Zero commute, more study

No crossing the Liluah overbridge, no auto-changing at Bandhaghat or Bally Halt, no GT Road North traffic during workshop hours. The roughly 90 minutes saved per class day goes back into practice, homework and rest.

THE NEIGHBOURHOOD

## A railway-workshop town that already thinks in systems

Liluah is a town built around precision. For nearly 145 years its Carriage & Wagon Railway Workshop has shaped not just rolling stock but generations of families — engineers, fitters, mechanics and supervisors who live in the colony on the workshop side, split from eastern Liluah by the Howrah–Bardhaman line and stitched back together by the overbridge. Children here grow up around tolerances, schedules and systems that have to work the first time. That is exactly the mindset great code rewards.

What we love about teaching Liluah families is that the engineering instinct is already there. A parent who maintains a railway carriage to the millimetre understands immediately why a misplaced semicolon breaks a program, and why testing matters. We simply give that instinct a new tool. The same logical, methodical thinking that keeps the Damodar Valley Corporation and the Eastern Railway running translates, almost effortlessly, into Python loops, Java classes and trained machine-learning models.

And because it is all live online, none of this costs a commute. No timing a walk over the overbridge between trains, no auto-change at Bandhaghat or Bally Halt, no sitting in GT Road North traffic during shift-change hours. The child opens a laptop at home, joins a small live batch with a real engineer, and the roughly 90 minutes that would have gone to travel goes to learning instead. RD Mall, Liluah Bazaar and Belur Math may be minutes away, but the best classroom is the one with no journey at all.

## An honest comparison for Liluah parents

### Where live online wins

- No travel over the Liluah overbridge or through GT Road North traffic — roughly 90 minutes saved every class day.
- Small live batches of 3–8 with a full-time engineer, not a crowded coaching-centre room.
- Your child shares their own screen, so the teacher sees and fixes mistakes in real time.
- Same premium teacher whether you're on the workshop side, Belur edge or Bally edge — location never limits quality.

- Easy to record-keep, revisit notes and continue projects between classes on your own laptop.
- Flexible rescheduling and 1-on-1 options that a fixed-location centre simply cannot match.

### When offline still wins (honest)

- A child who genuinely cannot self-start on a screen may need a parent nearby for the first few weeks.
- Very young learners sometimes focus better with a physical mentor in the room.
- Homes with unreliable internet or no quiet corner can find live sessions harder.
- Pure peer socialising — the chatter of a physical classroom — is reduced online.
