Will coding help with JEE and the CBSE, ICSE or PSEB board exams?

+

Yes. Coding strengthens the exact logical reasoning, decomposition and problem-solving that JEE, NTSE and Olympiads reward, and it directly supports the Computer Science, IT and NEP-2020 AI content taught under CBSE, ICSE and the Punjab School Education Board (PSEB). What's the best age to start coding?

+

Age 8 is ideal to begin with visual, block-based coding (Scratch). For text-based languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards works best. We run separate tracks for kids (8–12), teens (13–18) and college/working learners (18+), so the starting point always matches the child.

Do you prepare Jalandhar & Doaba college students for IT jobs and placements?

+

Yes — our college track is placement-focused: 200+ DSA problems, mock interviews, a MERN full-stack build and an AI/Data-Science portfolio. It's built for engineering and degree students from Jalandhar, Phagwara, Kapurthala and Nakodar targeting service majors, product companies, the Mohali–Chandigarh IT belt and the global remote roles they can do from home in Punjab.

What equipment does my child need?

+

Just a laptop or desktop and a stable internet connection — a good broadband or 4G/5G link works perfectly across Jalandhar and the Doaba towns. A computer (rather than a tablet) is best for hands-on practice. Minimum: Windows 10 / macOS, 4GB RAM. All the software we use is free, and our team helps with setup before the first class.

+

Yes. Weekday batches run 4 PM–8 PM (after school) and weekend batches 9 AM–6 PM (Sat & Sun). Jalandhar children juggle school, tuition and family commitments, so we work around your schedule. Classes are an hour long, twice a week. For NRI families in other time zones, we'll find a slot that works.

Yes — intensive camps run during the summer break (May–June) and winter break (Dec–Jan), letting a child finish a full course in 4–6 weeks.
