## From zero to real AI projects

There is no leap of faith here, only a clear climb. Every teen starts where they are and moves up one solid level at a time.

- **Level 1 - Code with confidence:** Python from the ground up. Logic, loops, and data - the toolkit every AI project is built on.
- **Level 2 - Make data talk:** Clean, explore, and visualise real datasets. Learn how machines find patterns humans miss.
- **Level 3 - Train your first models:** Build, train, and test real machine learning models. Watch your code learn from examples.
- **Portfolio - Ship and show off:** Finished projects your teen can demo, explain, and put on a college application or internship pitch.

## What your teen will actually build

Not toy worksheets that get thrown away. Real, working AI that does something, that your teen can run in front of a teacher, a parent, or an admissions officer and explain exactly how it works.

- **An AI chatbot** - a conversational assistant that understands what people type and responds in a way that makes sense.
- **An image classifier** - a model trained to look at a picture and recognise what it sees, the same idea behind face unlock and self-driving cars.
- **A recommendation mini-app** - the engine behind every "you might also like", built from patterns in data.
- **A small machine learning model** - take a real dataset, frame a real question, and train a model to predict an answer.

Every project becomes a portfolio piece - real work, neatly documented, ready to share for college applications, scholarship essays, and that first internship.

Real, working projects they can show and explain. **With AI everywhere, is this still worth it for my teen?**

Your teen leaves with real projects and genuine judgement, not just the ability to ask a chatbot questions.
