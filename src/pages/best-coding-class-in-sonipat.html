A working game, a live website, a small app the child can hand you and say "I made this." When admissions interviews and scholarship panels ask what a student has done, that beats one more number on a marksheet.

Will coding help with the CBSE, ICSE and Haryana Board exams?

+

Yes, on two fronts. First, the school paper itself: our Python and Java work maps onto the Computer Science and Informatics Practices syllabi in CBSE, ICSE/ISC and the Haryana Board, plus the NEP-2020 AI units. Second, the thinking: the same step-by-step reasoning that coding builds is exactly what JEE, CUET, NTSE and the Olympiads test. It helps the marksheet and the mind together.

What's the best age to start coding?

+

Around 8 is the sweet spot for visual, block-based coding (Scratch); for typed languages like Python and Java, 12–13 onwards suits most children. But there's no late — we teach right through college and working adults too. The honest answer for a Sonipat family is simply: the earlier the habit forms, the more natural it feels later.

Do you prepare Sonipat college students and engineering aspirants?

+

Yes — the 18+ track is built around real interviews: 200+ DSA problems, timed mock interviews, a MERN full-stack build and an AI/Data-Science portfolio. Jindal Global, DCRUST Murthal and IIIT Sonepat — who want to land remote and product-company roles without uprooting to a metro.

What equipment does my child need?

+

A laptop or desktop and a steady internet line — that's the whole list. A computer beats a tablet for real practice; anything running Windows 10 or macOS with about 4GB RAM is plenty. The coding software itself costs nothing to download, and our team walks you through the setup before the very first class.

What are the class timings? Do you have weekend batches?

+

Plenty of choice. Weekday slots run 4 PM–8 PM after school, and weekend batches run 9 AM–6 PM on Saturday and Sunday. Between board tuitions and entrance coaching, Sonipat children are busy — so we fit the class around their week, not the other way round. Each session is about an hour, twice weekly.

You'll already know a lot from the free demo. Even so, give it the first paid week — and if your child still isn't enjoying it, write to us within that week and we return the fee in full, no awkward questions. We'd genuinely rather you leave happy than stay reluctantly; a child who wants to be in class is the only kind worth keeping.

We do. Intensive camps run in the summer break (May–June) and the winter break (Dec–Jan), letting a child wrap up a full course in roughly 4–6 weeks.
