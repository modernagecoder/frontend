# Online math tutor for 5th grade

**The 20-second answer:** a good grade 5 tutor completes the fraction story (all four operations, built on the number line), extends place value through decimals so 0.3 x 0.4 stops producing 1.2, and closes elementary gaps before grade 6 assumes them.

## Why grade 5 is the hinge

Every idea from grades 3-4 reports for duty here, and grade 6 changes the subject entirely: ratios, rates, negative numbers and expressions assume fractions and decimals are finished business. The dangerous illusion is the rule-follower who looks fine until the first problem that bends the pattern.

## Signs a 5th grader needs help

Fraction addition is a memorized dance (butterfly method only); 0.3 x 0.4 = 1.2; "dividing makes smaller" is gospel; right numbers, wrong operation in word problems; volume and area interchangeable; PEMDAS recited but misapplied; no estimation instinct; middle-school worry has started; grades fine but fragile.

## The complete grade 5 map (Common Core aligned)

- Operations and Algebraic Thinking: order of operations with grouping understood, writing and interpreting expressions, paired patterns graphed.
- Base Ten: place value to thousandths, powers of 10, comparing and rounding decimals, multi-digit fluency, two-digit divisors, all four decimal operations with models.
- Fractions: add/subtract unlike denominators and mixed numbers, fractions as division, multiplying fractions, scaling intuition, dividing with unit fractions.
- Measurement and Data: volume by unit cubes and formulas (including composites), unit conversion, line plots with fractions.
- Geometry: first-quadrant coordinate plane, classifying 2D figures in hierarchies.
