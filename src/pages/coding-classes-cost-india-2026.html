Behind the number

What you are actually paying for

1

Marketing budgets

The biggest Indian edtech brands spend enormously on ads and sales teams. A meaningful slice of a Rs 9,000 fee is buying the ad that found you, not the teacher.

2

Sales-call pressure

High-priced packs need aggressive counsellors. If the trial ends in a two-hour closing call, the price includes that machinery.

3

Live depth versus content libraries

Recorded video with occasional mentor check-ins costs less to run but teaches less. A full live interactive hour is the premium product.

4

Class length and frequency

Some programmes count 40 to 55 minute sessions as classes. Compare monthly taught minutes: 8 full hours is 480 minutes of real teaching.

5

Packs versus monthly

Rs 29,500 packs lock you in before you know the teaching fits. Monthly billing keeps the school accountable every four weeks.

6

What is bundled

Maths in the same school, recordings for revision, project reviews and a consistent mentor are worth real rupees when included.

Honest answers

Frequently asked questions

What is a fair price for kids coding classes in India?

For genuinely live, small-batch teaching in 2026: roughly Rs 1,500 to Rs 3,000 a month for group formats and Rs 4,000 to Rs 8,000 for serious 1-on-1 is defensible. Above that, you are usually funding marketing and sales machinery rather than better teaching. Below Rs 1,000 a month usually means recorded content or very large cohorts.

Why do big brands charge Rs 9,000 or more a month?

Customer acquisition. Television ads, cricket sponsorships, app-install campaigns and large sales teams are all recovered from fees. The teaching hour underneath is often comparable to, and sometimes thinner than, what focused schools deliver at a fraction of the price.

Are course packs worth buying?

Be careful. Rs 29,500 upfront for a pack prices in months of classes before you know whether the teaching suits your child. Monthly billing keeps every school honest: if the depth drops, you can leave. We deliberately sell only monthly plans for exactly this reason.

Do any schools teach coding and maths together?

Very few, and it is our core design. The maths behind the code is what separates a copy-paste coder from a real problem solver, so we teach both in one school, with the same mentor culture, from Class 1 to college and beyond.
