## The problem we solve

Maths is the one subject where last year's gap sinks this year's grade. If algebra never
clicked, the next year feels impossible and a capable teen decides they're "not a maths
person." Cramming makes it worse — enough is memorized to survive a test, understanding never

## A real worked example: why anything to the power of zero is 1

Look at the powers of 2 counting down — each step divides by 2:

- 2⁴ = 16, 2³ = 8, 2² = 4, 2¹ = 2
- 2⁰ = 2 ÷ 2 = **1**

The step from 2¹ to 2⁰ must give 2 ÷ 2 = 1 — the only value that keeps the pattern
consistent (and it works for any base, so x⁰ = 1). A teen who sees this never forgets it, and
meets a powerful idea: rules are forced by consistency, not invented.

## Why a coding school teaches maths

The thinking behind good code is the thinking behind good maths: **decomposition**,
**abstraction** (an algebra function and a code function are the same idea), and **debugging
your reasoning**. A teen who learns maths as logic walks into CS, engineering and economics —
