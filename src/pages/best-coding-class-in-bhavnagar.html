A City That Already Does Global, Industrial Work — By the Sea

Bhavnagar isn't a small town that needs coding "imported". On the Gulf of Khambhat it dismantles ships from every ocean and reforges their steel, makes salt at industrial scale, and runs serious marine science. Coding is simply the next shipyard — and a Bhavnagar child can work it without leaving the Saurashtra coast.

World's #1

ship-recycling yard — Alang

Just down the coast, Alang is the planet's largest ship-breaking and recycling yard. Vessels from across the world end here — and their steel is reborn. Rebuild and renew is the local instinct.

Marine science

CSMCRI — salt & the sea

Bhavnagar is a major salt producer and home to CSMCRI, the CSIR Central Salt & Marine Chemicals Research Institute. A city that already turns seawater into chemistry and research.

Princely

heritage of the Gohils

A Gohil-dynasty capital whose Maharaja Krishnakumarsinhji was the first ruler to merge his state with independent India — a city used to leading, planning and giving the future its due.

Where Gandhi studied

Samaldas College & the University

A young Mohandas Gandhi studied at Samaldas College here, and Maharaja Krishnakumarsinhji Bhavnagar University carries the scholar tradition forward. A coding career can be launched from this very soil.
